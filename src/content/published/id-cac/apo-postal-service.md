## What

APO (Army Post Office) is a US-side mail system that treats your Wiesbaden address as domestic for shipping purposes. Mail and packages sent to your APO address travel through DoD's Military Postal Service to the Wiesbaden MPO, then to you via on-post mailboxes or dependent distribution.

You get an **APO address** when you arrive. Your sponsor or S1 assigns you a CMR (Community Mail Room) box number or similar.

## APO address format

Standard:
```
Your Name
Unit 29623 Box [your box number]
APO AE 09005-9623
```

Alternative with specific dependents:
```
Your Name
CMR 445 Box [number]
APO AE 09054
```

**Note**: "AE" = Armed Forces Europe. "09005" etc. are US-side zip codes that DoD MPO uses. Germans don't recognize them.

## What APO can receive

- **First-class mail** from US domestic senders: arrives in 5-10 business days
- **Priority Mail** from USPS: 7-14 days
- **Priority Mail Express**: 5-7 days (premium pricing)
- **FedEx / UPS ground**: typically route via US carriers; APO is usually their limit. Verify at ship-time.
- **Amazon US**: ships direct to APO as domestic. Most Amazon deliveries land in 5-10 business days via USPS.
- **Most US online retailers**: ship to APO if their software recognizes the format. About 80% of major US retailers do.

## What APO cannot receive

- **Perishables**: prohibited by USPS APO rules
- **Aerosol, lithium batteries (without restrictions)**, hazmat
- **Oversized packages**: limits around 20 lbs / 108" combined dimensions
- **Packages from outside the US**: APO is US-domestic-only. You can't ship yourself a package from Amazon.de to your APO.

## What about packages from Germany / EU?

You **need a German address** for:
- Amazon.de deliveries
- DHL / Hermes (German carriers)
- UPS Germany local
- German mail (*Briefe* and *Päckchen*)

### If you live on-post
Some on-post addresses work for German carriers; most don't. Packages may get rejected at the gate.

**Workaround**: use a Packstation (DHL). Create a DHL account, pick a Packstation location (many in Wiesbaden), and ship to that Packstation. Retrieve with a code. Free.

### If you live off-post
Your German address works for everything. Just use it.

## PO Box / Community Mail Room (CMR)

### CMR
Community Mail Room is a physical mailbox on Clay Kaserne or Hainerberg where your mail lands. Pick up by entering your box combination.

- Located at the Postal Services building, Clay Kaserne
- Boxes assigned at in-processing
- Keep your CMR combination private (it's like a PIN)
- Check mail regularly; uncollected packages go to "holding" after a few days

### Package pickup
Large packages don't fit in CMR boxes. You get a slip in your box directing you to the Postal Services counter for pickup. Bring the slip and your CAC.

Peak hours at the postal counter: **11:00-14:00 on weekdays** and after-work 16:00-17:00. Mornings 08:30-10:30 are quieter.

## Sending mail

### To the US
- **Drop at the Postal Services counter**; stamps sold at counter
- USPS rates: First-class from Germany to US $1.55 or so for a letter; packages via Priority Mail Express are fastest
- **APO-to-US** is treated as domestic on the US side, so forwarding fees and delivery speeds are normal-US, not international

### To Germany / Europe
Your on-post Postal Services does NOT ship to German addresses efficiently. Use:
- **DHL via German Post**; off-post Deutsche Post / DHL location (there are several in Wiesbaden); small parcels €5-€20 depending on weight
- **German Post stamps**: buy at any dm or Rossmann; standard domestic letter €0.85, postcard €0.70

## Amazon tips

- **Amazon US**: shipping to APO is the default once your account has the APO address. Works like a domestic US address.
- **Amazon.de (German Amazon)**: shipping to APO *doesn't work*; German Amazon uses German carriers who don't recognize APO. Ship to your off-post German address, a Packstation, or an on-post package concierge service.
- **Amazon Prime**: works for both US and German Amazon independently if you have separate accounts. Worth considering two accounts if you want both.

## On-post delivery concierge services

Some contractors on post offer "German-package-to-CAC-holder" relay services; you ship to their German address, they deliver to you on post. Ask in the USAG Wiesbaden Facebook group or ACS for current providers. Cost is usually €5-€15/package.

## Gotchas

- **APO is slow compared to stateside.** "5-10 business days" can stretch to 2 weeks during peak (Christmas). Order in advance.
- **Packages from China via AliExpress / Temu** typically don't ship to APO. Use a Packstation or German address.
- **Some US retailers block APO**; Lululemon, Nike, many cosmetics companies. Use a forwarding service (Shop&Ship, myUS) or check if they'll ship to a non-APO address.
- **Lithium batteries** are restricted; you can't ship a new iPhone to APO from a US retailer that strictly enforces hazmat rules. Amazon usually works around this; smaller retailers may not.
- **Bringing in prescription meds**; APO receives most prescription meds from US pharmacy. Large quantities may be flagged by customs on inspection; bring a doctor's letter.
- **Vehicles ordered via AAFES arrive at Clay Kaserne loading dock**, not APO. Separate process.
- **Mail forwarding to US when PCS-out**; set up USPS "change of address" from your APO to your next stateside address 2 weeks before PCS. Some mail forwards automatically; some doesn't. Check what arrived for a while.
- **Lost packages from APO**; file claims with USPS at `usps.com/help`. Can take 30 days.
- **Customs inspections**; about 1 in 50 APO packages get opened by customs. If yours does, you'll see a re-taped box. Nothing to worry about.
