## What

The garrison's main gym is the **Wiesbaden Sports and Fitness Center** on Clay Kaserne, BLDG 1631. Cardio deck, free weights, machines, functional-fitness turf, two group-class studios, locker rooms, and a small pro shop. Soldiers, Reservists, Guard, retirees, DoW Civilians, and Family members use it for free. Authorized patrons can bring up to two guests per visit.

Army MWR offers a **24/7 access program** at some garrison gyms for patrons who complete a registration and after-hours safety brief. Ask the front desk whether Wiesbaden's program is currently active and how to enroll; availability shifts with staffing and facility policy.

Unit intramural sports (flag football, soccer, basketball, softball, volleyball) run out of the same office — sign up through your unit sponsor or the intramural coordinator.

## Where

- **Main gym** — Clay Kaserne BLDG 1631 (front desk, classes, weight floor, cardio)
- **Auxiliary space** — the same complex houses an indoor court and the intramural office
- **Hainerberg** — no dedicated MWR gym; Hainerberg housing residents drive or bus to Clay
- **PT tracks** — outdoor running tracks at Clay and an informal loop around Hainerberg housing

Parking is on-site; show your access pass at the Clay gate.

## When

- **Mon–Fri** 0500–2030
- **Sat/Sun** 0830–1700
- **Federal and training holidays** 0830–1700 (reduced staffing; classes usually cancelled)
- **24/7 access** may be available through the MWR after-hours program — confirm at the front desk

Classes (cycling, HIIT, yoga, strength) typically run at 0545, 1130, and 1730 weekdays; weekend schedule is lighter. The class calendar at the MWR page is authoritative.

## Contact

Contact details in the frontmatter. For class enrollment or personal-training inquiries, call the main line or walk up to the front desk.

## Who can use the gym

- Active duty (Army, Air Force, Marine Corps, Navy, Coast Guard)
- Reserve and National Guard
- Retirees and 100% service-connected-disabled veterans
- DoW Civilians (appropriated and NAF) while stationed OCONUS
- Authorized contractors with approved ID
- Family members of the above
- Up to **two guests per visit** with an authorized patron; guests sign in with photo ID

## Intramural sports

The intramural office runs unit-level competitions across USAG Wiesbaden, feeding into the **Army Commander's Cup** (basketball, flag football, soccer, softball, volleyball). Seasons follow the calendar — flag football in fall, basketball in winter, soccer and softball in spring/summer.

Sign up through your unit's MWR rep or the intramural coordinator at BLDG 1631. Registration windows close early; miss it and you sit out the season.

The **All-Army Sports Program** (higher level, inter-service) accepts soldier-athlete and coach applications yearly — ask intramurals for the packet.

## Off-post alternatives

Wiesbaden has dozens of private gyms, most with contract commitments:

- **Fitness First**, **McFit**, **clever fit** — chain gyms, €20–40/month, 12-month contracts common
- **Wiesbaden city pools** (*Opelbad*, *Freizeitbad Mainzer Straße*) — great summer options, day tickets
- **Taunus trails** — hiking, trail running; bus or train from Wiesbaden Hbf

Most German gyms run cash- or SEPA-direct-debit billing. Contracts without a clean cancellation clause are notorious — read before signing.

## Gotchas

- **Weekend hours cut deep**: 0830–1700 is shorter than most US on-post gyms. Plan weekend lifts early.
- **24/7 access is not automatic.** If available, it requires MWR enrollment and an after-hours safety brief. Ask at the front desk your first week if you'll need odd-hours workouts.
- **Guests need photo ID**; a German driver's license works, but base access beyond the gym still requires the authorized patron to escort.
- **Kids**: youth policies vary by program. Children generally need a parent present or enrolled in the youth fitness track; ask at the desk before bringing a teenager to lift.
- **Lockers** are day-use only; anything left overnight gets cut off by facility staff.
- **Classes fill**: pre-register at the desk or through the MWR app for popular classes (Saturday morning yoga, weekday HIIT).
- **Personal training** costs extra; first consult is usually free, follow-up sessions are per-hour at MWR rates.
- **Physical requirements for intramurals**: DA 7566 or current PHA for Soldiers; waivers for civilians case-by-case.
