## What

Three different on-post retailers, three different purposes:

- **Commissary (DeCA)**; groceries, US brands, tax-free, cash-price + 5% surcharge
- **PX / Exchange (AAFES)**; non-grocery: electronics, clothing, home goods, sporting, food court, car buying
- **Shoppette**; mini-mart with gas station (tax-free US fuel), snacks, tobacco, alcohol

Hainerberg has the main commissary + PX complex; Clay Kaserne has a smaller PX. Off-post grocery shopping (Rewe, Aldi, Edeka, Lidl) is necessary for things the commissary doesn't stock (German specialty foods, fresh bread, some produce).

## Where

### Hainerberg main complex
- **Commissary**: 0900-1900 Tue-Fri, 0900-1800 Sat-Sun, closed Mon
- **PX / Exchange**: 0900-1900 Mon-Sat, 1000-1700 Sun (varies by season)
- **Food court / Restaurants**: Charleys Philly Steaks, Popeyes, Baskin-Robbins, Arby's, Manchu Wok (rotates)

### Clay Kaserne PX (smaller)
Limited selection, gas station, food court with fewer options. Fine for lunch runs but not your main shop.

### Shoppettes
- Clay Kaserne Shoppette
- Hainerberg Shoppette
Both have gas pumps with the **AAFES gas card**; fuel at tax-free US-indexed prices (~$3.50/gal as of 2024 vs ~$7/gal on the German economy).

## How the math works

### Commissary
Prices are close to US-stateside but you pay:
- **5% surcharge** at checkout (no sales tax, but this covers Commissary operations)
- **$5 minimum charge** or so on card purchases; check
- Bring a **bag for bagging**; commissary baggers accept tip in USD

### PX / AAFES
Prices are USD, tax-free. Comparable to stateside retail (sometimes cheaper on sale items). Big-ticket items (appliances, TVs) sometimes better via AAFES with PCS-specific promotions.

### Shoppette gas
You need an **AAFES Ration Card** to purchase fuel. Issued once per tour at the ID Card Center or Ration Control Office. Limit is based on vehicle type and dependents; usually plenty for family use. Violating ration limits (reselling fuel) is a serious offense.

## When you still need to shop off-post

### Groceries the commissary doesn't do well
- Fresh bread (German bakery is unbeatable)
- Fresh German produce (seasonal, superior to air-freighted US equivalents)
- German dairy (Quark, specialty cheeses, local butter)
- Wine (German, French, Italian; commissary carries mostly California)
- Anything specific to a region (Asian grocery, Middle Eastern, Indian)

### German supermarkets nearby
- **Rewe**; full-service supermarket, everywhere in Wiesbaden
- **Aldi / Lidl**; discount chains, cheap basics
- **Edeka**; upscale supermarket with more variety
- **dm / Rossmann**; drugstores with health/beauty; cheaper than PX beauty
- **Müller**; similar to dm but larger
- **BIO / Reformhaus**; organic/health food stores

### Online ordering
- **Amazon.de** delivers to on-post housing addresses (APO + German address both work in different cases; test both)
- **Amazon.com** works but shipping is slow (10-14 days standard)

## Shopping the PX strategically

- **Before PCS-out**: stock on US things you'll miss; peanut butter (good stuff), ranch dressing, American-style BBQ sauce
- **Electronics**: PX is competitive on laptops, TVs, cameras; less competitive on phones (buy unlocked from Germany for easier SIM swaps)
- **Clothing**: depends on brand. Think NEX/PX-specific brands for the tour.
- **Furniture**: AAFES furniture is fine for a 3-year tour, not long-term. Consider IKEA (Eschborn, 20 min) for the PCS.
- **Car buying**: AAFES Exchange offers US-spec Fords, Jeeps, BMWs, etc. Pricing is below dealer MSRP. Convenient for factory-order-then-ship.

## Gotchas

- **Commissary is closed Monday.** The line on Tuesday morning is brutal. Tuesday afternoon or Wednesday morning is quieter.
- **Some commissary products are "export-restricted"** (mostly dairy and some produce); if you see signs, respect them.
- **Gas prices at Shoppette track US prices, not German.** One reason to keep your POV.
- **Ration cards can be revoked** for abuse (reselling, exceeding limits). Don't.
- **PX and Commissary both enforce ID checks** at entry and sometimes at checkout. Carry your ID card everywhere.
- **Exchange prices vary across USAREUR**; sometimes cheaper at Ramstein, sometimes at Kaiserslautern. Online AAFES (shopmyexchange.com) ships to Wiesbaden.
- **Food court cash**: some kiosks don't take cards. Keep a few dollars / euros.
- **Commissary has better meat than most German grocery chains** for US cuts (brisket, specific ribs). German butcher shops (*Metzgerei*) are better for German cuts.
