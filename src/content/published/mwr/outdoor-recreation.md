## What

**Outdoor Recreation (ODR)** is the MWR program that sells the tour most of Europe offers to U.S. tourists for twice the price. Two things live under one roof:

1. **Guided trips.** Weekend bus trips to Amsterdam, Paris, Prague, Oktoberfest, Christmas markets, ski weekends in Austria, Normandy battlefield tours, Rhine river day trips. Price usually covers transport, lodging, and sometimes entrance; food is your own.
2. **Gear rental.** Camping (tents, stoves, packs), bikes, kayaks, ski/snowboard packages, party gear (folding tables, grills, moon bounces), roof racks and cargo carriers. Rates are well below German rental companies.

ODR is where you go when you want to travel but haven't figured out how to yet. The bus trips handle the logistics; your only job is show up at the Clay lot with an overnight bag.

## Where

- **Office + counter** — Clay Kaserne; shares space with the Sports and Fitness Center complex area
- **Bus trips depart** from the Clay Kaserne parking lot (exact location emailed with confirmation)
- **Rental pickup/return** — Clay, during staffed hours only

There is no Hainerberg or Mainz-Kastel ODR counter. Everything routes through Clay.

## When

Office hours are seasonal — open more in spring/summer, trimmed in winter. Typical steady-state:

- **Tue–Fri** 1000–1800
- **Sat** 0900–1700
- **Sun/Mon** closed

Bus trip season picks up in March and runs through mid-December. The Christmas-markets tours in late November and early December book out faster than anything else on the calendar.

Gear rental is year-round but inventory shifts with the season (skis in winter, kayaks in summer).

## Contact

Contact details in the frontmatter. The MWR landing page posts the current trip calendar and rental rates; the counter takes reservations in person or by phone.

## How the trips work

1. **Scan the calendar** — usually posted on the Wiesbaden MWR portal and the USAG Wiesbaden Facebook page. Trips list price, dates, what's included, and who it's pitched at (adults only, family-friendly, etc.).
2. **Book in person** or by phone. Deposit secures the seat; balance due before departure.
3. **Cancellation window** — cancel before the cutoff (usually 14 days out) for a refund; cancel after and you forfeit the trip cost.
4. **Show up at the Clay lot** at the listed time. Bus loads. Guide does a headcount and briefs the weekend.
5. **You come back on the same bus.** Leaving a trip early (to meet friends in Paris, say) is allowed but you lose the return seat.

### Trips that tend to sell out

- **Oktoberfest** (Munich) — sells out within hours of posting
- **Christmas markets** (Nuremberg, Strasbourg, Cologne) — early November releases
- **Paris Christmas** — December
- **Normandy D-Day** (usually early June) — sells out 4–6 weeks in advance
- **Ski Austria / Switzerland** — January/February
- **Amsterdam flower parade** — spring

Sign up for the MWR mailing list so you hear about new trips within the hour they post.

## Gear rental inventory (indicative)

- **Camping**: 2- to 6-person tents, sleeping bags and pads, camp stoves, lanterns, coolers, packs
- **Biking**: adult and kids' bikes, trailers, helmets, car racks
- **Watersport**: kayaks (sit-on-top and sit-in), SUPs, paddles, PFDs, dry bags
- **Winter**: ski and snowboard packages (board, boots, poles/bindings), snowshoes, sleds
- **Party / hosting**: folding tables and chairs, pop-up canopies, grills, party games, moon bounces
- **Trailer and cargo**: utility trailers, roof cargo boxes

Rental terms are by the day or by the week; you sign for the gear and pay on return. Bring your ID card and a credit card for the security hold.

## Gotchas

- **Book trips early.** If a trip hit your radar and posted within the week, buy now; don't wait to coordinate with friends.
- **Cash vs card** — ODR takes card for most things, but some in-person payments still want euros. Carry both when picking up rentals.
- **Gear returns are strict.** Dirty camping gear or a wet tent returned wet gets a cleaning fee. Dry and brush off before returning.
- **Bus trip headcounts are firm.** Miss the departure and the bus leaves; there's no "wait ten minutes."
- **Passports and ID**: international bus trips cross Schengen borders without stops, but carry passport anyway — spot checks happen. Some trips (Switzerland, Norway) need passport because they're outside Schengen or have separate EEA rules.
- **Kids and bus trips**: some trips are adults-only (beer-focused Oktoberfest, certain wine tours). Check the listing before booking with kids.
- **Cancellations happen.** Low enrollment can cancel a trip 2 weeks out; book any airline/train tickets around a trip with that risk in mind.
- **Gear reservations** aren't always taken; popular items (kayaks in July, skis in January) go first-come. Call ahead to confirm availability.
