## What

Kids' dental care under TRICARE in Wiesbaden splits across three systems:

1. **ARHC Dental Clinic** — sees Active Duty primarily; Family members and DA Civilian dependents on a space-available basis. Good for routine cleanings and basic restorative work for kids when appointments are available.
2. **TRICARE Dental Program (TDP)** administered by **United Concordia** — separate enrollment-based coverage for Family members; reimburses off-post German dental care for kids at a defined cost-share.
3. **DoDEA school dental screenings** — annual screenings at DoDEA schools; routed to parents with recommendations.

Most kids end up using **off-post pediatric Zahnärzte (pediatric dentists)** for their regular care, with ARHC as a backup or for specific treatment, and TDP reimbursing the off-post costs.

Orthodontics, sedation dentistry, and complex treatment almost always go off-post to German specialists.

## Where

### ARHC Dental
Located at the clinic on Clay Kaserne. Pediatric dental appointments available when staffing allows — call ahead to confirm.

### Off-post pediatric Zahnärzte (Wiesbaden)
- Several pediatric dental practices in Wiesbaden Innenstadt and outlying districts
- Many describe themselves as **Kinderzahnarzt** (children's dentist) or have kids' wings within a general practice
- Orthodontists are separate specialists (*Kieferorthopäde*); need a referral or direct search

### Orthodontic practices
Orthodontics is widely available in Wiesbaden; many practices have English-speaking orthodontists, especially near base.

### Hospital-based pediatric dentistry
- **HSK Wiesbaden** and **St. Josefs-Hospital** — for cases requiring sedation or hospital-level care
- **Mainz University Hospital** — for complex craniofacial or specialized pediatric dental needs

## When

- **ARHC routine** — book 2–8 weeks out; cleanings and exams
- **Off-post German Zahnarzt** — 1–4 weeks for routine; same-week for urgent
- **Orthodontic consults** — 2–6 weeks for first visit; treatment plans over months to years
- **DoDEA school screenings** — annual, typically fall semester
- **Emergency dental** — same day on-post (ARHC) or at any German emergency dental clinic (*Notdienst*) off-post

## TRICARE Dental Program (TDP) for families

### Who's eligible
- Family members of Active Duty (spouses, children)
- National Guard and Reserve family members on active orders
- Not automatically enrolled — must **enroll** via the United Concordia portal
- **Enrollment window after PCS** — 30 days to enroll without a waiting period

### Coverage
- **Routine cleanings and exams**: 100% covered, twice yearly
- **Basic restorative** (fillings, extractions): 80% covered typical; 20% cost-share on network charge
- **Orthodontics** (kids under 21): 50% covered up to a lifetime maximum (~$1,750)
- **Emergency care**: covered per plan terms

### OCONUS specifics
Overseas TDP care uses **Active Dental** network (United Concordia's international dental provider list). You:
1. See a dentist — ideally network, can be any licensed dentist
2. Pay up front (most OCONUS dentists don't direct-bill US insurance)
3. File the claim with United Concordia via the portal
4. Receive reimbursement at the covered percentage

See [Specialty referrals deep dive](/entries/medical/specialty-referrals-deep-dive) for general TRICARE Overseas navigation; dental is on a separate program but the administrative logic is similar.

### Orthodontic coverage specifics
TDP orthodontic benefit covers up to 50% of the treatment cost, with a lifetime maximum around **$1,750 per child**. German orthodontic treatment typically costs €3,000–7,000 over 2–3 years. Expect a significant cost-share beyond what TDP covers. Budget accordingly; financing plans are common at German orthodontic practices.

## DoDEA school dental screenings

Each DoDEA school coordinates an **annual dental screening** — typically fall semester. A dentist or hygienist visits the school, screens kids, and sends home a form indicating:

- **No issues detected** — continue routine care
- **Further evaluation recommended** — parents should schedule a cleaning and exam
- **Urgent care needed** — parents should schedule immediately

This is **screening, not treatment**. It doesn't replace regular dental visits. Parents still book cleanings twice a year.

## Finding a pediatric dentist off-post

Tactics that work:

1. **Ask DoDEA parent networks** (school Facebook groups, PTA) — specific recommendations by name
2. **Ask ACS or the SLO** — occasional provider lists, especially for kids with special needs
3. **Search the United Concordia Active Dental network** for TDP in-network dentists in the Wiesbaden area
4. **Visit the practice** before committing for an ongoing kid relationship — look for kid-oriented waiting room, pediatric-friendly staff, dental-anxiety management

### English capability
Most Wiesbaden pediatric dentists serving the base community speak functional English. Some are native speakers. Confirm at scheduling.

### What a German kids' dental visit looks like
- **Child-focused exam chairs** — most pediatric offices have colorful rooms and patient staff
- **Less gas (nitrous) use** than US — many German dentists prefer behavioral management over sedation for routine work
- **Fluoride treatments** — administered routinely, similar to US
- **Preventive orientation** — German dentistry emphasizes prevention heavily; expect sealants, regular cleanings, and dietary advice
- **Radiographs** — digital and used sparingly; some practices take them annually, some less

## Sedation dentistry for kids

Some kids need sedation or general anesthesia for dental work (severe dental anxiety, special-needs kids, very young kids with complex decay). Options:

- **Office-based sedation** (oral sedation, nitrous) — available at some Wiesbaden pediatric practices
- **Hospital-based general anesthesia** — at HSK or St. Josefs for surgical cases or severe cases
- **LRMC pediatric dental** — available through TRICARE referral for military-covered dental surgery

TDP may cover sedation with prior authorization; confirm before the appointment.

## Orthodontics for kids

- **First orthodontic consultation** typically around age 7–9 (German and US norms similar)
- **Phase 1 orthodontics** (interceptive, before all permanent teeth) — varies by case
- **Full orthodontics** typically starts age 11–13
- **Treatment length**: 18–36 months typical
- **Braces types**: metal, ceramic, aligners (Invisalign and similar) — all available in Wiesbaden
- **Payment**: German orthodontists often offer monthly payment plans; TDP reimburses per their schedule
- **German grade coverage** (*Kieferorthopädie-Grade*) — German insurance covers different levels based on severity grade; irrelevant for TRICARE but may affect what the orthodontist quotes

## Emergency dental

- **On-post during ARHC Dental hours** — walk into the clinic, explain emergency
- **After-hours / weekends** — look for German dental Notdienst (*Zahnärztlicher Notdienst*); typically a rotating on-call clinic, listed on aponet.de or at any Apotheke window
- **True emergencies** (jaw fracture, major trauma) — go to a hospital emergency room; dental injuries often need oral surgery available at HSK or LRMC

Emergency dental for kids: common scenarios include knocked-out teeth (time-critical; save tooth in milk or saline, see dentist within 30 minutes), severe pain from decay (usually abscess or pulpitis; Apotheke can provide pain relief short-term), dental trauma from sports.

## Gotchas

- **TDP is NOT automatic enrollment.** Families must enroll after PCS. Missing the 30-day window creates a waiting period.
- **Orthodontic lifetime maximum** is low relative to German treatment costs. Plan to cover €2,000–4,000 out-of-pocket for a typical case.
- **TDP OCONUS reimbursement is slower than CONUS**. Expect 4–8 weeks on claim processing for overseas dental.
- **Keep every receipt** and itemized treatment plan; TDP audits claims and needs documentation.
- **"Dental emergency" definition** varies — bleeding gums isn't; a broken tooth with exposed nerve is. Know the difference before driving to the ER.
- **On-post ARHC dental slots for kids fill fast.** If your kid has a scheduled issue (orthodontic consult, suspected cavity), don't wait for ARHC — go off-post if ARHC is booked.
- **Sedation consent** and paperwork for kids — bring both parents' signatures for general anesthesia cases; German practice is stricter than US.
- **Vision is separate** — TRICARE has a separate vision benefit; dental and vision don't share eligibility or claims.
- **DoDEA screenings generate a flag, not a referral.** Screen results tell you to see a dentist; they don't book the appointment.
- **Two cleanings a year is the TDP standard.** Some kids need more with high-decay risk; additional cleanings require justification and may not be fully covered.
- **German Zahnärzte don't always use nitrous for kids.** If your kid specifically needs nitrous for anxiety, ask upfront — not all practices offer it.
- **Orthodontic retainers post-treatment** have their own cost; budget for replacement retainers during the growth years.
- **Lost retainers** — most practices charge €100–300 for replacement; some plans cover once.
