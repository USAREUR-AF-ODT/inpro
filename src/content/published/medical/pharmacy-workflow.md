## What

Two pharmacy systems coexist at Wiesbaden:

1. **ARHC Pharmacy**; on-post pharmacy at the Army Health Clinic (ARHC) Wiesbaden. Fills TRICARE prescriptions from ARHC providers and many outside providers. **No copay for active duty, low/no copay for family members depending on plan.** Drive-through and walk-in windows; online refill system.
2. **German Apotheke**; private pharmacies throughout Wiesbaden. Fill German prescriptions (*Rezept*) and most US TRICARE prescriptions for occasional or urgent needs. You pay up front, then file for TRICARE reimbursement (most prescriptions reimburse in full for eligible patients).

For 90% of routine medications, ARHC is the answer. For the other 10% (specialty drugs ARHC doesn't stock, after-hours or weekend needs, German-prescribed medication), you use a German Apotheke.

## Where

### ARHC Pharmacy (Clay Kaserne)
Co-located with the Army Health Clinic. Drive-through window for refills; walk-in window for new prescriptions. Hours and location confirmed at the clinic front desk.

### German Apotheken (city-wide)
- **Hainerberg-adjacent options**; several within a 10-minute drive
- **Wiesbaden Innenstadt**; dense cluster of Apotheken in the pedestrian zone
- **Kurhaus / Ring-Apotheke / Bahnhofsapotheke**; typical pharmacy names; signs usually have a red *A* or the classic red "*Apotheke*" placard
- **Notdienst rotation**; the after-hours on-call pharmacy rotates nightly across the city; current Notdienst listed at [aponet.de](https://www.aponet.de/) or at the window of any closed pharmacy

### Mail-order pharmacy (TRICARE)
- **Express Scripts TRICARE Pharmacy Home Delivery**; some maintenance medications delivered to APO addresses; set up through milConnect or the Express Scripts portal
- **Generally slower than ARHC walk-in**; useful for stable maintenance prescriptions

## When

- **ARHC Pharmacy hours**: Mon-Fri duty hours (typically 0800-1600 with a lunch closure; confirm at clinic)
- **ARHC refills**: submit via the online refill system 24-72 hours ahead; pick up during hours
- **German Apotheken**: Mon-Sat 0800-1830 typical; many close for a midday break (1230-1430); Sundays closed except Notdienst
- **Notdienst**: 24/7 rotation; specific Apotheke and address listed at aponet.de or on the window of the nearest closed pharmacy

## Contact

Contact details in the frontmatter. For medication questions:

- **ARHC pharmacist**; during clinic hours, walk up to the pharmacy window
- **Nurse advice line** (TRICARE); 24/7, international number printed on TRICARE card

## How ARHC pharmacy works

1. **New prescription**; ARHC provider writes; Rx appears in the pharmacy queue. Walk up or use the drive-through within 24 hours to pick up. No paper prescription usually needed; they have it electronically.
2. **Prescription from another military provider** (e.g., LRMC, Ramstein); usually appears in the ARHC system automatically within hours. If not, call ARHC.
3. **Prescription from a civilian or German provider**; bring the paper prescription to the ARHC pharmacy window. Pharmacist reviews and may substitute a formulary-equivalent drug.
4. **Refills**; submit online at the ARHC refill portal or via the Defense Health Agency mobile app. Allow 24-72 hours. Drive-through is fastest for pickup.
5. **90-day supplies**; available for maintenance medications. Saves trips; standard for hypertension, cholesterol, diabetes, thyroid, etc.
6. **Formulary**; ARHC stocks the TRICARE formulary plus some additions. Non-formulary drugs require a prior authorization or substitution.

### Not stocked at ARHC
- Some brand-name drugs (generic substitution is common and usually fine)
- Specialty / biologic medications (require coordination with TRICARE specialty pharmacy)
- Controlled substances in non-standard strengths
- German-formulation equivalents

If ARHC doesn't stock, they'll tell you where to fill (local Apotheke with paper prescription, mail-order, or LRMC for specialty).

## How a German Apotheke works

1. **Bring a prescription**; either a German *Rezept* (from a German doctor) or a TRICARE prescription on paper (electronic US prescriptions don't transfer to German systems).
2. **Hand it to the pharmacist** at the counter; they look up the medication in the German formulary and sometimes recommend a local-equivalent if exact match isn't available.
3. **Pay up front**; German Apotheken do not bill TRICARE. You pay in cash or by card, get the receipt, and file for reimbursement.
4. **Keep the receipt and the *Beleg*** (detailed breakdown). TRICARE needs the itemized record, not just the payment receipt.
5. **File for TRICARE reimbursement** via the Express Scripts / TRICARE Overseas claims portal. Typical turnaround: 2-4 weeks.

### Typical prescription costs at an Apotheke
- **Generic antibiotic** (7-day course); €5-15
- **Brand-name hypertension meds** (30-day); €15-40
- **Controlled substances** (narcotic pain, sleep, ADHD); higher and require German *Betäubungsmittel-Rezept* (special controlled prescription); tighter rules than US
- **Insulin and biologics**; €30-120 per unit depending on type

German prescription structure caps patient out-of-pocket at ~€10 per prescription for residents on statutory insurance (*gesetzliche Krankenversicherung*), but you pay full German retail as a TRICARE patient.

## OTC differences you'll notice

German pharmacy practice is **pharmacist-gated for more medications than the US**. Drugs you can grab off the shelf at Walmart require you to ask a pharmacist in Germany, and the pharmacist will advise before selling:

- **Ibuprofen and paracetamol (acetaminophen)**; available OTC in small quantities; larger doses (400 mg+ or 50+ count) are pharmacist-only
- **Pseudoephedrine** (Sudafed, Claritin-D); tightly controlled; often unavailable
- **Diphenhydramine** (Benadryl); not sold OTC in Germany in the same way; alternatives exist but are different compounds
- **Aspirin**; OTC
- **Mucolytics** (cough medications); pharmacist-mediated
- **Nasal steroid sprays**; OTC in Germany
- **Antihistamines** (Claritin, Zyrtec equivalents); available in Apotheken with a quick consult
- **Homeopathic remedies**; widely stocked (Germany has a deep homeopathic tradition); pharmacists will offer these if you ask for a cold remedy
- **Vitamins and supplements**; in drugstores (*dm*, *Rossmann*) without consultation; Apotheke consultation for anything in therapeutic doses

### Drugs available OTC in Germany but Rx-only in the US
- **Diclofenac gel** (Voltaren); topical pain; OTC in Germany
- **Loratadine, cetirizine**; OTC
- **Hydrocortisone cream** (low dose); OTC
- **Ranitidine / famotidine**; OTC

### Drugs Rx-only in Germany that are OTC in the US
- **Pseudoephedrine**; tightly controlled
- **Melatonin**; Rx-only in most strengths
- **Higher-dose ibuprofen / naproxen**; Rx-only in larger doses
- **Certain sleep aids**; Rx-only

## TRICARE reimbursement mechanics

1. **Pay at the Apotheke**, get receipt + *Beleg* (detailed)
2. **Log in to the Express Scripts TRICARE portal**, select "OCONUS reimbursement"
3. **Upload receipt and prescription documentation**
4. **Receive reimbursement** to your bank account (DFAS-linked account for Active Duty; own account for retirees) in 2-4 weeks
5. **TRICARE Prime** typically reimburses 100% for covered medications; **TRICARE Select** has some cost-share
6. **Keep copies** of everything; denials happen and the appeal is documentary

See [TRICARE overseas](/entries/medical/tricare-overseas) for the broader TRICARE coverage picture.

## Controlled-substance specifics

- **US C-II controlled substances** (Adderall, oxycodone, etc.) require special handling. ARHC may fill with limitations; off-post requires a German doctor's *Betäubungsmittel-Rezept*.
- **Bringing controlled substances from the US**; legal in reasonable personal-use quantities with prescriptions and DD Form 2807 or equivalent documentation. Going through customs, declare them.
- **Long-term controlled Rx**; plan with ARHC 60+ days before supplies run out; these are the most likely drugs to cause PCS-related access problems.

## Gotchas

- **Pseudoephedrine is not easily available.** Plan alternatives for cold/allergy management, or bring a supply from US (declare at customs).
- **Generic substitution at ARHC is the norm.** If a specific brand matters medically, have your provider note "DAW" (dispense as written) and expect to justify it.
- **Weekend and evening medication runs** are harder in Germany than the US. Notdienst pharmacies exist but add an hour and require the app or signage to find.
- **Prescriptions expire**; German Rezept is usually valid 30 days; ARHC prescriptions follow US timelines.
- **Drive-through at ARHC saves time.** Submit the refill online, hit drive-through at lunch; done in 5 minutes.
- **German Apotheken don't always stock** exact US equivalents. The pharmacist will offer the closest German formulary match; verify it's therapeutically equivalent before walking out.
- **Reimbursement isn't automatic.** File each claim through Express Scripts; expect a few to get kicked back and require supporting documentation.
- **Pets**: German Apotheken fill human prescriptions only. Pet medications go to vet clinics.
- **Infant formula and baby medications**; usually available at drugstores (*dm*, *Rossmann*) without prescription; German standards may be different from US.
- **Homeopathic alternatives** are offered casually. Ask specifically for conventional medicine if you don't want homeopathic.
- **Expired medications from the US**; disposal at any German Apotheke's take-back bin; don't flush or landfill.
- **During a German doctor visit**, you typically get a paper Rezept. The doctor does NOT email it to the pharmacy; you carry it.
