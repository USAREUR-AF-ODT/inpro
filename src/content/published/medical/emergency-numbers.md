## What

The single most important page to save on your phone before you land. Germany and the US military use different emergency numbers — and on-post vs. off-post use different ones too.

## Where to dial

### German side (works anywhere off-post)
- **Fire / Ambulance** — `112` (Europe-wide emergency)
- **Polizei (police)** — `110`
- **Poison Control (English-speaking)** — `+49-911-398-2451`
- **Poison Control (German)** — `+00-800-444-88444`

### On-post (Wiesbaden)
- **Military Emergencies (Police, Fire, Ambulance)** — dial `110` from a post phone, or `+49-611-143-548-0110` from civilian
- **On-post fire/ambulance (alt)** — `117` DSN or `(0611) 705-117`
- **Military Non-Emergency Police** — DSN `548-7777` / `+49-611-143-548-7777` (Clay Kaserne, Bldg 1001/1004)
- **Fire Non-Emergency** — DSN `337-5315/5883` / `+49-611-705-5315`
- **Installation Operations Center (IOC)** — DSN `548-3003` / `+49-611-143-548-3003`

### 24-hour hotlines
- **Child Abuse Hotline** — DSN `548-7777` / `+49-611-143-548-7777`
- **Domestic Violence Response** — `+49-162-297-5625`
- **Sexual Assault Response (SHARP Victim Advocate)** — DSN `53-SHARP (74277)` / `+49-162-296-6741`
- **Veterans/Military Crisis Line** — `988` or `001-844-702-5495`
- **American Red Cross (emergency family message)** — `+1-877-272-7337`

## When to use which number

- **Anywhere in Germany, life-threatening**: `112`
- **On-post, life-threatening**: `110` (from on-post phone) or drive to the ARHC or off-post hospital
- **Non-emergency crime on post**: `548-7777`
- **Crisis/mental health**: `988` (Veterans/Military Crisis Line)

## Contact

See the numbers above. Put them in your phone's emergency contacts **before** you fly.

## Gotchas

- **You cannot call DSN from your civilian cell phone.** Use the civilian equivalent listed above.
- **US 911 does not reach German services.** 112 and 110 are the emergency numbers.
- **The 2024 Welcome Packet uses different numbers than the current crisis page in places** — the live garrison page at `home.army.mil/wiesbaden/contact/crisis-phone-numbers` is more current. Numbers here reflect the current page.
- **When calling German emergency services from a German mobile, a location usually pops on their screen automatically.** State your language ("English, please") first — dispatchers in Hessen generally handle English.
- **Off-post hospital ERs require ID and may ask for payment up front.** Keep your TRICARE Overseas card handy; you'll be reimbursed for covered emergency care.
