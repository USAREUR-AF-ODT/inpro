## What

The Religious Support Office (RSO) on Clay Kaserne is the single coordination point for on-post religious services, chaplain counseling, and faith-specific community groups. They cover the common Christian traditions and help connect members of other traditions (Jewish, Muslim, Buddhist, Hindu, LDS, etc.) to off-post or nearby-garrison resources.

## Where

**RSO — Clay Kaserne.** The chapel complex on Clay hosts Sunday services; specific service times rotate by season and chaplain availability. Check the garrison bulletin or call the RSO for current times.

**Hainerberg Chapel** — smaller chapel, used for midweek services and small-group events.

## Services typically offered on-post

- **Protestant (general)** — Sunday service, often ecumenical
- **Catholic** — Sunday Mass, Holy Day observations
- **Liturgical Protestant** (Lutheran/Anglican/Methodist) — depends on assigned chaplain
- **Jewish** — High Holy Days observed; regular Shabbat services coordinate with Heidelberg Jewish community for Active Duty
- **Youth and teen ministries** — CYS-chapel coordination
- **Vacation Bible School** — summer weeks
- **Military weddings and funerals** — coordinated through RSO

Service schedules shift with PCS cycles (new chaplains = sometimes new service styles). Don't assume the schedule you see in January holds in August.

## Chaplain counseling

Chaplains hold **100% confidentiality privilege** — conversations cannot be compelled by command or military courts. This is different from behavioral health, which has limits on confidentiality. If you want a pastoral ear without chain-of-command exposure, the chaplain is the appointed-person.

Any Soldier or Family member can request a chaplain visit — call the RSO (main line) and ask.

## Off-post English-language options

Wiesbaden has an active international community. Off-post English-language churches include:

- **International Baptist Convention** (Wiesbaden) — English-speaking
- **St. Augustine's Anglican Church** (Wiesbaden)
- **Catholic parishes** (some offer occasional English Mass; check with the diocese)
- **Jewish Community of Wiesbaden** (*Jüdische Gemeinde*) — German-primary but welcomes English-speakers
- **Muslim community** — multiple mosques in Wiesbaden, some with English Friday prayers
- **LDS** — Wiesbaden Ward under the Frankfurt Stake
- **Buddhist / Hindu** — smaller communities, mostly Frankfurt-based (30 min by train)

The RSO keeps a current list of off-post contacts and can refer you.

## Life events

- **Marriage**: military chaplains can perform ceremonies for Active Duty and dependents at the Clay or Hainerberg chapel. German marriages also require a civil ceremony at the Standesamt (city marriage bureau) — the religious ceremony has no legal effect under German law.
- **Baptism / dedication**: coordinate with the assigned chaplain for your tradition.
- **Funerals**: the RSO coordinates memorial services on-post; repatriation of remains is handled through Survivor Outreach Services / Casualty Assistance.
- **Divorce support**: chaplains often run marriage-support programs and grief groups post-divorce.

## Gotchas

- **Chaplain assignment changes** — don't assume the pastor/priest you met on arrival is still there 18 months in. Introduce yourself to the new one.
- **German federal church tax** (*Kirchensteuer*) is 8–9% of income tax assessment for registered members of major German churches. If you register (Anmelden) and mark "Protestant/Catholic" on the form, the tax can apply. **SOFA-status personnel are generally exempt** from Kirchensteuer — but the Bürgeramt may still record religion if you provide it. Better to leave the religion field blank at Anmeldung unless you specifically want German-church membership.
- **English-language services off-post fill fast** on major holidays (Christmas Eve, Easter). Arrive 30+ min early for reserved seating at the internationally-staffed ones.
- **Religious garb / observance during duty** — chaplains advise on accommodations; request in writing through the chain.
- **Holiday observances** — Good Friday, Easter Monday, Whit Monday, and Corpus Christi are federal holidays in Hessen (state where Wiesbaden sits). Post and much of the economy closes.
