## What

Four broad paths to work as a military spouse in Wiesbaden. Each has different rules, pay scales, and setup time.

1. **Appropriated Fund (AF / GS)**; civilian government jobs on US installations
2. **Non-Appropriated Fund (NAF)**; MWR, Commissary, CYS positions; easier hiring, similar benefits
3. **On-post contractor / private-sector US companies**; US tax structure, often remote
4. **German economy**; working for a German employer; requires Arbeitserlaubnis (work permit)

Each has trade-offs. Many spouses combine multiple approaches across a 3-year tour.

## Path 1; Appropriated Fund (GS)

Federal civilian jobs advertised on USAJobs. Wiesbaden has a substantial AF workforce: V Corps, 66th MI, 5th Signal, INSCOM, USAREUR HQ, and garrison support roles.

**Pros**:
- Standard federal pay scale (GS) with locality pay
- Federal benefits (FEHB health, FERS retirement, leave, TSP)
- Career ladder across PCS tours (military spouse hiring preference)
- Work on secure/cleared environments common; clearances transfer

**Cons**:
- Hiring is slow (3-6 months common)
- Available openings depend on current hiring authority and command need
- Security clearance process for some jobs adds months

**How to start**:
- Set up a USAJobs profile pre-PCS
- Use the **Military Spouse Hiring Preference** on applications (one-time use per PCS)
- Register with the **Wiesbaden CPAC** (Civilian Personnel Advisory Center); they staff AF hiring actions
- ACS ERP offers federal-resume workshops

## Path 2; Non-Appropriated Fund (NAF)

NAF jobs are "private"-within-DoD; MWR staff, Commissary cashiers, CYS teachers, bowling alley employees, lodge staff. Hiring is faster, pay lower than GS, benefits partial.

**Pros**:
- Fast hiring (weeks, not months)
- No clearance required for most positions
- Flexible hours common
- Foot-in-door for military-adjacent work

**Cons**:
- Pay scale lower than GS
- Benefits partial (health insurance optional; retirement different)
- Positions often part-time or seasonal

**How to start**:
- NAF jobs are posted on USAJobs or directly on the Wiesbaden MWR site
- Walk-in resume drops at CYS, Commissary, and MWR are common
- ACS ERP helps format NAF-appropriate resumes

## Path 3; US private sector (remote)

Plenty of spouses work US jobs remotely while in Germany. The legal pieces get tricky but the pattern works:

**What works cleanly**:
- Your US employer keeps you on their US payroll, maintains your US-state tax status, handles your health insurance (or drop onto military TRICARE)
- You pay US federal income tax on the income; Germany typically won't tax you if you're SOFA-status and don't establish a German tax residency
- Work is done from your German residence
- **You DON'T need a German work permit** if your only employer is US-based, your work is done for US-based clients, and you're not a German tax resident

**What gets messy**:
- Some US employers require in-state employment (you must work from a specific US state)
- Taxation gets complex if you work for multiple US employers or have German-source freelance work
- German tax law might apply if you stay past 6 months per year with SOFA gone or with substantial German business activity

**How to start**:
- Talk to your US employer **before** PCS; many allow it, some require formal remote-work agreements
- **MyCAA** (My Career Advancement Account); DoD grant up to $4,000 for portable career training (counseling, teaching, healthcare)
- **MSEP**; Military Spouse Employment Partnership; US employer network that explicitly hires military spouses; many are fully remote

## Path 4; German economy

The hardest but most interesting path. You can legally work for a German employer with the right paperwork.

**Requirements**:
- Valid passport + SOFA stamp + Anmeldung at a Wiesbaden address
- **Arbeitserlaubnis (work permit)** from Stadt Wiesbaden's Ausländerbehörde (Foreigners Office); free for SOFA-dependent spouses under the SOFA agreement, processed in 2-6 weeks
- German tax ID (Steueridentifikationsnummer); issued to residents after Anmeldung
- German residency status (SOFA-dependent status qualifies)
- **German language ability**; varies by job; some international companies work entirely in English

**Common fields for English-speakers**:
- Wiesbaden has a substantial international business community (Henkell Freixenet, SGS, Hessenstiftung)
- Frankfurt (35 min by train) is Germany's financial capital; finance, consulting, international law, tech
- Teaching English at private language schools, Berlitz, or tutoring
- International schools (teacher, admin)
- US contractor companies hiring for US installations (translate as "on-post German economy" in some cases)

**Pay scale**: German salaries are typically lower than US for equivalent roles, especially outside finance and tech. Benefits (health insurance, generous leave) are better.

## ACS Employment Readiness Program (ERP)

The first stop for spouse employment. Services:
- **Resume review**; both US federal and European private-sector formats
- **Interview coaching**
- **Job board access**; curated listings from Wiesbaden employers, virtual hiring events
- **Career counseling**; 1:1 assessment of your career goals given the PCS cycle

Call ACS at **DSN 548-9201** and ask for the Employment Readiness Program manager.

## Military-spouse-specific benefits

- **MyCAA**; $4,000 toward licenses, certifications, or associate-degree programs. Must be spouse of Active Duty E-5 and below, or WO1-CW2, or O1-O2.
- **PCS taxation**; PCS-related spouse unemployment has specific tax treatment; talk to MilTax
- **Spouse hiring preference**; one-time federal hiring preference per PCS move
- **MSEP partners**; Amazon, Microsoft, Booz Allen, many others explicitly hire military spouses remotely

## Gotchas

- **SOFA status ≠ work authorization.** Your SOFA stamp is for residency, not employment. German jobs require the separate work permit.
- **MyCAA has time limits** and must be used on DoD-approved programs. Plan ahead.
- **German employers pay less** than US equivalents for most non-finance roles. Expect 60-80% of a US salary for similar work.
- **Two-income German tax planning** is different from US; the German spouse's income is taxed separately (unless married filing jointly under *Ehegattensplitting*, which requires German residency for both). Consult a tax pro.
- **Maternity leave** in Germany is extensive (14+ months possible); don't assume you can return to a US-based job after a long German parental leave without updating your employer.
- **Credentials portability**; US professional licenses (nursing, teaching, law) don't transfer automatically to Germany. Requires re-certification in German, usually.
- **Remote US work from Germany**; some US states (California, New York) have aggressive state-tax rules that continue to apply if you "work from home" in Germany. Consult state tax guidance.
- **Freelancing in Germany** requires a *Freiberufler* tax ID and additional paperwork; not the lightest lift.
