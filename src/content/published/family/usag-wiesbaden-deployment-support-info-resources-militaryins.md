## Content Intro

## Deployment Support Overview

Deployment can be a challenging phase of the military lifecycle, but it’s important to know that you don’t have to go through it alone. The military provides a variety of resources and support programs — ranging from educational briefings to morale calls — for your loved ones, children and service members to ease the stress of deployment. Military members may be assigned to a specific installation but support may come from local communities, Yellow Ribbon events or the installation itself.

## Subtopics

Family Deployment Support

**Mission Ready Families = Mission Ready Soldiers**

Family readiness impacts unit readiness. In addition to a supportive chain of command, an effective Family Readiness Group is vital, especially during deployments.

FRG volunteers and unit leadership are provided with the tools that are needed to maintain a successful and effective FRG through training and an endless supply of resource materials.

The Mobilization and Deployment Program can provide training, briefings, workshops and resources such as:

- Care Team training and resources
- Children and youth deployment support and training
- Army civilian and family member deployment support and training
- Pre-deployment support and training for soldiers and families
- Rear detachment commander training and resources
- Reintegration Preparedness training and workshops
- Trauma in the unit training
- Operation Ready
- Emergency preparedness

Contact your Mobilization and Deployment Program Manager at ACS for a complete list of classes and trainings and to request or schedule one.

Unit briefings are available to prepare soldiers and family members for deployment readiness.

Location: Building 7790, Mississippi St., Hainerberg Housing, Wiesbaden.

Telephone: 0611-143-548-9201/9202 or DSN 314-548-9201/9202

Family Readiness Group (FRG)

Family readiness impacts unit readiness. In addition to a supportive chain of command, an effective Soldier Family Readiness Group is vital, especially during deployments.

SFRG volunteers and unit leadership are provided with the tools that are needed to maintain a successful and effective SFRG through training and an endless supply of resource materials.

Please contact the ACS Mobilization and Deployment Program Manager for a complete list of classes and trainings and to request or schedule one.

Unit Briefings are available to prepare Soldiers and family members for deployment readiness.

Additional resources are available under the “Managing Deployment” section at [www.myarmyonesource.com.](https://installations.militaryonesource.mil/military-installation/usag-wiesbaden/military-and-family-support-center/www.myarmyonesource.com)

**Soldier Family Readiness Group Leader Training**

This training includes the key factors and operational guidance essential to leading and managing an effective and successful SFRG.

Required for SFRG leaders.

**Soldier Family Readiness Group Key Contact Training**

This training includes instruction on using phone trees, maintaining rosters, using community resources, effective communication techniques and procedures to use when contacting soldiers and family members. Required for SFRG key contact position.

**Soldier Family Readiness Group Informal Fund Custodian Training**

This training reviews detailed information about SFRG funds management including the authorized and restricted uses of SFRG funds, fundraising, acceptance of unsolicited gifts and donations, and the duties of an SFRG Informal Fund Custodian.

**Command Family Readiness Representative Training**

Command Family Readiness Representative (CFRR) course is designed to prepare Soldiers who are appointed as CFRRs to assist with the operational, logistical, and administrative aspects of the SFRG. As a liaison between the command and the SFRG volunteer the CFRR needs to know the ins and outs of the SFRG and how their role can assist both the command and the volunteers.

**Casualty Response (CARE) Team Training**

This training provides information and guidance on what the role of the SFRG is during a crisis or emergency. The course includes setting up an SFRG CARE team and coordinating community resources to provide assistance during a unit crisis or emergency.

**Please call to speak with the Mobilization and Deployment Program Manager at ACS for training schedule and for an appointment at ACS 0611-143-548-9201/9202 or DSN 548-9201 or 9202.**
