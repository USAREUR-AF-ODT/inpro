## What

Child and Youth Services (CYS) is the Army's end-to-end childcare and youth programming. In Wiesbaden, CYS covers:

- **Child Development Center (CDC)**; full-day infant through pre-K care
- **School Age Care (SAC)**; before/after-school + summer care for K-6
- **Middle School & Teen Program**; drop-in programming, trips, sports
- **SKIES Unlimited**; lessons and clubs (piano, gymnastics, martial arts, art)
- **Youth Sports**; seasonal leagues (soccer, basketball, baseball, etc.)
- **Family Child Care (FCC)**; in-home care by certified providers
- **Hourly Care**; drop-off for errands, appointments

Registration, fees, and scheduling all route through a central office; several programs use a web system (**WebTrac** or the current CYS portal). Register your kids as early as possible; popular slots fill fast.

## Where

CYS has multiple facilities across Wiesbaden's footprint:

- **Hainerberg CDC**; infant to preschool
- **Aukamm Youth Center**; school-age care + teen programming
- **Clay Kaserne Youth Sports fields**; seasonal league play
- **Parent Central Services (PCS) Office**; registration and administration. Parent Central is the central front-door for CYS paperwork; their exact building shifts with post layout changes.

Phone: **DSN 548-9197** or **+49-611-143-548-9197** (ACS main routes to CYS).

## Registration; the first step

Before your kid can use any CYS program, they must be registered. Steps:

1. **Prepare paperwork**:
   - DA Form 5225 (children's medical form) signed by a licensed provider
   - Immunization records (translated to English if originally German)
   - Sponsorship verification (CAC + orders)
   - Income documentation (sponsor LES; spouse paystub if working)
   - Emergency contact info for 2 people besides the sponsor/spouse
2. **Create a WebTrac account** at the CYS portal (link through the garrison MWR site)
3. **Submit registration** online or at Parent Central Services
4. **Pay registration fee** (usually waived for initial signup)

The registration itself takes 1-2 weeks to process. **Don't wait until you need childcare**; register as soon as you arrive.

## Childcare; CDC and FCC

### Child Development Center (CDC)
- Full-day, center-based care
- Infant (6 weeks+), toddler, preschool programs
- Typically 0700-1730, Mon-Fri
- Subsidized fees based on total family income
- **Expect a waitlist**; infant spots are hardest to get

### Family Child Care (FCC)
- In-home care by certified providers (currently in their own house)
- More flexible hours than CDC
- Smaller groups (usually 2-6 kids)
- Subsidized similarly

**FCC is often faster to start than CDC.** If you need care NOW and CDC has a waitlist, FCC is the workaround.

### Income-based fees (rough 2024 numbers)
- Infants/toddlers full-time: $200-$800/month depending on rank and income
- Preschool full-time: $180-$700/month
- Hourly care: $5/hour

## School-Age Care (SAC)

For K-5 kids during:
- **Before school** (0600-0800)
- **After school** (school pickup until 1800)
- **Summer camp** (full-day, June-August, fills in April)
- **Seasonal camps** (winter break, spring break)

The SAC vans pick up at DoDEA Wiesbaden schools. Register your child through WebTrac and CYS coordinates the pickup.

## SKIES Unlimited

Lessons and enrichment. Categorized:
- **SKIES Arts**; piano, guitar, dance, art classes
- **SKIES Sports**; martial arts, gymnastics, swim
- **SKIES Enrichment**; robotics, chess, tutoring

Individual class fees (~$10-$30/hour) + any necessary equipment. Check the SKIES catalog quarterly.

## Youth Sports

Seasonal:
- **Fall**: soccer, flag football
- **Winter**: basketball, wrestling
- **Spring**: baseball, track
- **Summer**: swim team, tennis

Registration fee (~$50/season) + uniform. Volunteers (often parents) coach. Practice 1-2 evenings per week, games Saturday.

Wiesbaden Youth Sports is inter-USAREUR; older age brackets travel to other Army garrisons for games.

## Teen Center

For middle schoolers (6-8th grade) and high schoolers (9-12th grade):
- Drop-in programming
- Supervised homework space
- Organized trips to Rhineland sites
- Teen dances, movie nights
- **Middle School Club**; free drop-in, open to military teens

## EFMP and CYS

CYS accommodates children with EFMP medical or educational flags. Coordinate through:
1. Parent Central; note EFMP at registration
2. EFMP liaison at ACS; matches your child to program support
3. School-based IEP (for SAC pickup); SLO coordinates

Inclusive program design; specific accommodations vary by child's needs. Talk to Parent Central specifically about your child's EFMP category.

## CYS and DoDEA school calendars

- DoDEA has **10 weeks of summer break**, 2 weeks at winter break, 1 week spring break
- SAC runs during all these with **separate registration** (summer fills first)
- Camp registration opens ~6 weeks before each camp
- **Book summer camp in March or April**; it fills by May

## Gotchas

- **Registration is mandatory before any CYS use**; including the "free" drop-in teen center (they want the paperwork on file for safety).
- **WebTrac is clunky.** Give yourself 30 min for first registration; save passwords.
- **Medical forms expire annually.** Keep a renewal reminder.
- **Parent volunteer hours required** for some subsidized slots. Check the current policy when you register.
- **Late pickup fees at CDC** are aggressive; $10 per 15 minutes or similar. Leave work early if traffic is bad.
- **Sick-child policy is strict.** Any fever, vomiting, or rash means your kid doesn't attend for 24 hours symptom-free. Have backup care lined up; emergency care through CYS is limited.
- **Waitlists vary enormously.** Infants months, preschoolers weeks. Register at PCS arrival, not when you need care.
- **Summer camp theme changes yearly**; the sports camp you loved last year may not run this year. Re-check the seasonal catalog.
- **Two parents working; no auto-priority.** You don't get bumped to the top of the waitlist just because both spouses work. Family Child Care may be your bridge.
