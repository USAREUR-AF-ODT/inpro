## Household Goods Overview

A PCS or military move can be a challenge, especially if you have a lot of stuff. Breaking down your to-dos into more manageable pieces often helps. Here, you’ll find out how to handle household goods, manage your pet and guidelines on the shipping process.

Your installation [household goods/transportation office](https://installations.militaryonesource.mil/search?program-service=39/view-by=ALL) can provide assistance with understanding your entitlements and shipping your personal property. For information on shipping your vehicle, check out the [Personal Property Resources](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/resources/tools/personal-property-resources/) page on MilitaryOneSource.mil, or visit the [PCSmyPOV](https://www.pcsmypov.com/) website.

Arranging Household Goods Shipments

As soon as you receive your permanent change of station orders, it’s important to begin scheduling your move. The Defense Personal Property System, or DPS, is the online system you can use to request and manage your household goods shipment(s). To schedule your move, [log in to DPS](https://dps.move.mil/cust/) using a common access card, or CAC, or by obtaining a user ID and password through the DPS online system.

For more information, visit the [Moving Your Personal Property](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/moving-pcs/moving-personal-property/) page on MilitaryOneSource.mil.

Read [Arranging a Personal Property Shipment](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/resources/millife-guides/preparing-to-move-or-pcs/) to learn more about coordinating your shipment and review the [Personal Property Quick Reference Guide](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/documents/personal-property-quick-reference-guide-d1255/) for actions you can take to ensure a smooth move.

Learn more about [Your OCONUS Move: Planning, Resources and Tips](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/moving-pcs/living-oconus/a-service-family-checklist-for-moving-oconus/). Begin to plan what items will go in unaccompanied baggage, in your household goods shipment and in long-term, Non-Temporary Storage, or NTS, storage – check with your sponsor or overseas housing office to assist in determining what items to ship.

If you have an issue and would like to speak to someone, the Personal Property Activity, or PPA, is another resource available to assist during your move. You can speak directly with a fellow uniformed service member who can provide more information or help you work through any issues you encounter.

**Personal Property Activity**

**Call center:** 833-MIL-MOVE (833-645-6683)

**Hours:** Mon – Fri, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. CT

[Email the PPA Call Center](mailto:PCSCallCenter@mail.mil).

Pets

Moving with your pet includes researching airline requirements and quarantine restriction laws in your new location. Many airlines have requirements for size, weight, breed, number of animals, kennel construction, documentation and seasonal limitations. Small pets may be shipped on military flights, but availability and regulations frequently change. Contact your installation [household goods/transportation office](https://installations.militaryonesource.mil/search?program-service=39/view-by=ALL) to request specific country instructions. [Military OneSource](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/moving-pcs/plan-to-move/pcs-and-military-moves/) can make your move easier with helpful tips, resources and hands-on information related to [moving with pets](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/moving-pcs/plan-to-move/moving-with-pets/) and more.
