## What

You don't need a car in Wiesbaden. You might want one, but many Family members spend their entire tour without driving and don't regret it. The public-transit plus on-post shuttle combo covers daily needs.

## The Wiesbaden Shuttle Bus (free, on-post only)

The garrison runs a free shuttle that loops between Clay Kaserne, Hainerberg Housing, Hainerberg Exchange/Commissary, the Army Lodge, and Newman Village. Schedule is updated irregularly (check the garrison bulletin or the Welcome Packet PDF).

Approximate routes (confirm current schedule):
- **Clay Kaserne ↔ Hainerberg**; every 20-30 min during duty hours
- **Hainerberg ↔ Newman Village**; less frequent
- **Weekend shuttle**; reduced service

Stops are posted at each pickup location. No ticket, no fare; just show up.

## ESWE city buses (Wiesbaden municipal transit)

ESWE (Wiesbadener Verkehrsgesellschaft) runs city buses everywhere in Wiesbaden proper. Every housing area has bus stops within walking distance.

- **Single ticket**: €3.20 (adult)
- **Day pass**: €7.50
- **Monthly pass**: €80 (or included in Deutschlandticket; see below)
- **Kids under 6**: free with an adult

Key lines for US military:
- **Line 1**; Clay Kaserne ↔ city center ↔ Hainerberg
- **Line 3**; Hainerberg ↔ Newman Village ↔ downtown
- **Line 14**; Aukamm ↔ Bierstadt ↔ city center

Download **ESWE's app** or use **DB Navigator** (Deutsche Bahn app); both give real-time schedules.

## Deutschlandticket (€58/month); the power move

As of 2024-2025, the **Deutschlandticket** gives you unlimited rides on all regional trains, buses, and U-Bahn/S-Bahn across all of Germany for €58/month. Not the ICE/IC long-distance fast trains; but everything local, everywhere.

Pays for itself in less than 20 rides per month. Most Family members should have one.

- Buy through the **DB Navigator app**, **ESWE app**, or any major public transport operator
- Subscription auto-renews monthly; cancel anytime
- Not transferable; one person per ticket

This is the single best transit investment in Wiesbaden.

## Taxi; Taxi Wiesbaden (0611-444-444)

The only cab company authorized to pick up on-post. Flag a cab off-post and you probably can't get back onto Clay Kaserne without switching to this company.

**Process**:
1. Call 0611-444-444
2. Tell dispatch: "I need a taxi with a U.S. Installation Pass"; this is specific language they use
3. Give your pickup location (on-post building or gate)
4. Give your destination

Rates:
- Base fare: ~€3.50
- Per-km: ~€2.50
- Wait time: ~€0.50/minute
- Airport surcharge: ~€5

Typical fares:
- Clay Kaserne → downtown Wiesbaden: €15-€20
- Clay Kaserne → Wiesbaden Hbf: €12-€18
- Clay Kaserne → FRA (Frankfurt Airport): €70-€90

Uber and Bolt operate in Wiesbaden, but **not authorized on-post**. You can take them off-post to anywhere that's also off-post.

## Deutsche Bahn (DB); regional and long-distance rail

**Wiesbaden Hauptbahnhof** (main station) is 10 min from Hainerberg by bus, 15 min from Clay Kaserne.

Two train types:
- **Regional** (S-Bahn, RE, RB, IC): 0-2 hours, included in Deutschlandticket for most of them
- **Long-distance** (ICE, IC): 1-7 hours, separate ticket required

Frequent routes from Wiesbaden Hbf:
- Mainz Hbf: 15 min, €4 or free w/Deutschlandticket
- Frankfurt Hbf: 40 min, €8 or free w/Deutschlandticket
- Heidelberg: 1.5 hour, €20-€40 ICE or €25 regional
- Cologne / Düsseldorf: 2 hour ICE, €50-€90

Book ICE tickets 3 months out for Sparpreis deals (€25-€45 one-way to major cities).

## Bike culture

Wiesbaden has bike lanes and is mostly flat. Bike-share (nextbike) is everywhere. Many Family members keep a bike + a single car.

Bike routes:
- **Hainerberg ↔ Clay Kaserne**: 15 min, flat
- **Hainerberg ↔ downtown**: 20 min, mostly flat
- **Aukamm ↔ Clay**: 20 min, downhill to work, uphill home
- **Rheingau bike path** (along the Rhine): one of Germany's top bike routes; leave from Wiesbaden Biebrich

## Scooters and e-bikes

Tier, Bolt, and Voi all operate electric scooters / e-bikes in Wiesbaden. Download the app. Good for last-mile connections (train station to home). Not allowed on-post; park off-post and walk in.

## Driving alternatives for Families

- **One car, strategic use**; most families land here with one shipped POV. That's plenty.
- **Car-sharing (Share Now, Miles)**; operates in Wiesbaden; good for the occasional day you need a car
- **Rental from AAFES / Hertz**; good for weekend trips if you don't own
- **German rental from off-post**; often cheaper than AAFES; requires German or USAREUR license

## Gotchas

- **The on-post shuttle doesn't run to the airport.** Don't miss your flight assuming it does.
- **The AAFES taxi line can be busy** during PCS peak; call 20-30 min ahead of needed pickup time.
- **Sundays have reduced bus/shuttle service.** Plan grocery shopping Saturday; church/brunch via walk or taxi Sunday.
- **ESWE night buses (Nachtbus)** run hourly Fri-Sat after midnight; otherwise last bus is ~23:00.
- **ICE trains sell out for holidays** (Christmas, Easter, Oktoberfest). Book early.
- **The "Deutschlandticket on a child"**; kids under 6 ride free; 6-14 needs their own (subsidized €29/month option exists). Confirm current rules.
- **Airport shuttle from Wiesbaden to FRA**: Flibco and Hessen Express run regularly; ~€15 one-way, 40 min. Cheaper than a taxi if you're alone.
