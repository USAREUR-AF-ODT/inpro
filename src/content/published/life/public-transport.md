## What

Wiesbaden does not have a U-Bahn (metro). Local and regional transport runs on **ESWE buses** (city buses) and **S-Bahn and regional trains** (Rhein-Main transit). All of it lives under the **RMV** (Rhein-Main-Verkehrsverbund) fare network. Buy one RMV ticket, you ride anything in the zone.

The single best move for most families is the **Deutschlandticket** (also called the *D-Ticket* or colloquially *49-Euro-Ticket*, though the price rose to **€58/month in 2025**). It's a subscription that covers **all regional and local transit in Germany** — buses, trams, S-Bahn, regional trains — everywhere except ICE/IC/EC long-distance trains. For a family that commutes to Frankfurt, travels to nearby cities, or just doesn't want to deal with tickets each ride, it pays for itself fast.

## Where

### Key hubs
- **Wiesbaden Hauptbahnhof (Hbf)** — central train station; all S-Bahns, regional trains, ICE trains
- **Wiesbaden Bushof** (central bus station) — at Hbf; ESWE buses hub here
- **Mainz Hbf** — 10 minutes by S-Bahn; connects to Rhineland-Palatinate network
- **Frankfurt Hbf** — ~35 minutes by S1/S8/S9 S-Bahn or regional train; Germany's 3rd-busiest station, hub for long-distance and flights via FRA
- **Frankfurt Airport (FRA)** — S8/S9 direct from Mainz (with change) or regional train from Wiesbaden Hbf

### On-post bus routes
ESWE bus **line 15** (and others) serves Clay Kaserne and Hainerberg. AAFES runs **on-post shuttles** during peak PCS season between Lodge, Clay, and the Exchange complex.

## When

- **S-Bahn frequency** on Wiesbaden lines (S1/S8/S9 to Frankfurt via Mainz): every 30 minutes off-peak, every 15 minutes peak
- **Regional trains** (RB/RE to Frankfurt direct): hourly
- **ICE trains** from Wiesbaden Hbf: limited (most ICE service is from Frankfurt Hbf)
- **ESWE city buses**: every 10–20 minutes on major routes during the day; hourly evenings and Sundays
- **Night buses** (*Nachtbus*) — Friday and Saturday nights, limited routes
- **Sundays and holidays** — reduced schedule; some bus lines don't run

## Contact

- **RMV customer service** — rmv.de/en; the RMV app (iOS/Android) handles planning, tickets, real-time delays
- **Deutschlandticket** — subscribe through RMV, Deutsche Bahn, or a regional transit authority; auto-renew SEPA direct debit
- **ESWE customer center** — Wiesbaden Hbf; day tickets and paper passes

## How to ride

### Buying tickets
- **RMV app** — the default for most people; contactless, real-time schedule, works offline after sync
- **DB Navigator** — Deutsche Bahn app; also sells RMV tickets and long-distance
- **Ticket machines** at every station and major bus stop; English menus; cash and card accepted
- **Paper tickets** still work; some bus drivers sell single tickets, most don't

### Fare types
- **Single ticket** (*Einzelfahrt*) — €3–5 depending on zone
- **Day ticket** (*Tageskarte*) — ~€10 all-day, group tickets for families
- **Deutschlandticket** — €58/month, cancels monthly; cheapest option if you ride more than ~15 times/month
- **9-Uhr-Ticket** — cheaper variant valid after 0900 weekdays, all day weekends (regional only)
- **Hessen-Ticket** — day ticket covering all of Hessen, useful for day trips to Kassel, Frankfurt, Marburg

### Validating
Most RMV tickets are pre-validated when you buy them. Paper tickets need stamping in machines on platforms before boarding — forget this and you ride *schwarz* (ticketless), fine is €60+ if caught.

### On buses
- **Enter through any door** if you have a valid ticket
- **Single tickets** from the driver — enter through front door, announce destination
- **Cash** works on buses; tap card payments accepted on newer vehicles
- **Signal the stop** with the red button before your stop; German buses do not stop at every stop by default

## Key routes from Wiesbaden

- **To Frankfurt**: S1 via Mainz (45 min), S8/S9 via Mainz (45 min), RE direct (35 min)
- **To FRA (airport)**: regional train (RE) direct ~25–30 min from Wiesbaden Hbf
- **To Mainz**: S1/S8/S9 (10 min) — cheap option to connect to Rheinland-Pfalz network
- **To Koblenz**: RE (1 hr) — direct access to Rhine Valley
- **To Cologne**: ICE or IC (1.5 hr) — requires separate ticket (not D-Ticket)
- **To Berlin**: ICE 4.5 hr — separate ticket

## Driving vs. transit tradeoffs

- **Commuting to Frankfurt daily** — transit wins. Parking in Frankfurt is €20–50/day and traffic is bad.
- **Commissary run with 4 bags of groceries** — car wins.
- **Weekend day trip to Rüdesheim or Rhine Valley** — train is more fun; wine tasting without a designated driver.
- **Airport pickup** — depends; S-Bahn works fine if you don't have big bags.
- **Late-night return** (after midnight) — car wins; buses and some trains stop running.
- **Snow days** — trains outperform cars most winters.

## Gotchas

- **Strikes happen.** German rail/bus strikes hit a few times a year, usually with 24–72 hour notice. Check RMV or DB Navigator alerts before leaving.
- **Deutschlandticket is subscription-only** and auto-renews. Remember to cancel when you PCS out.
- **Deutschlandticket does NOT cover ICE/IC/EC.** Long-distance trains need separate tickets.
- **Kids ride free** on RMV up to age 6; age 6–14 reduced fare. D-Ticket is for one person only.
- **Bike on transit** — bikes are allowed on S-Bahn and regional trains with a bike ticket (*Fahrradkarte*), not during peak hours on some lines.
- **Eating on trains** is socially acceptable; loud calls or music are not. Use headphones and the quiet car (*Ruhebereich*) if available.
- **Ticket inspections are random** and unforgiving. "I didn't know I needed to stamp" gets you a €60 fine and a record.
- **FRA airport trains run into the night**, but the last S-Bahn from FRA to Wiesbaden is around 0130. After that, taxi or Uber from FRA to Wiesbaden is €80–120.
- **Apps work best with a German SIM or EU roaming**. RMV and DB are geo-lite and load schedule data without internet once you plan a trip.
- **Class 1 vs Class 2** on regional trains — most people ride 2nd class; 1st class on regional trains is rarely worth it unless you have a specific reason.
- **S-Bahn vs. U-Bahn vs. regional**: Wiesbaden has no U-Bahn. Frankfurt does. S-Bahn is regional/commuter; U-Bahn is city metro; regional trains (RE/RB) cover longer distances at fewer stops.
