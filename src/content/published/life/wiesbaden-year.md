## What

Germany has seasons, and Wiesbaden leans into them. This is a month-by-month calendar of what's worth doing, avoiding, or anticipating. Dates are approximate; check current calendars close to the date.

## January

**Quiet, cold, grey.** Post-New-Year's doldrums. Restaurants open but muted. Museums are open and uncrowded; best time for the Städel, Gutenberg, Jewish Museum.

- New Year's Day: public holiday
- Epiphany (6 Jan): public holiday in some German states (not Hessen)
- Berliner (jelly donut) is the local January food
- SAD is real; invest in a sunrise lamp

**Do**: indoor museums, Wiesbadener Theater, thermal baths (Kaiser-Friedrich-Therme), cross-country skiing in the Taunus if snow
**Skip**: outdoor markets

## February

**Still cold. Fasching / Karneval starts early-mid-month.** Fasching is a big Rhineland thing; Wiesbaden is quieter than Mainz / Cologne. Mainz has the major parades.

- Rosenmontag (Monday before Ash Wednesday): Mainz parade day
- Valentine's Day (14 Feb): German restaurants book up early
- Fasching season ends with Ash Wednesday

**Do**: Mainz Karneval events, traditional Rhineland food, ski weekends in Austria
**Skip**: outdoor dining; it's too cold

## March

**Spring starts. Wine tourism opens.** Rheingau wineries reopen their tasting rooms. Crocuses and snowdrops in the parks. Days get noticeably longer.

- Rheingau wineries open weekends starting mid-March
- Daylight savings begins last Sunday of March (EU spring forward)
- First *Maibock* (bock beer) of the season

**Do**: short Rheingau day trips, garden center visits (German families start spring planting)
**Skip**: multi-day outdoor travel (weather still unreliable)

## April

**Spring in full. Asparagus season begins (Spargelzeit) mid-April to late June.** White asparagus on every menu. Easter markets, Easter egg trees.

- Good Friday + Easter Monday: public holidays in Hessen
- Spargelzeit: order white asparagus at every German restaurant
- Walpurgisnacht (last night of April): bonfire festivals in the Taunus

**Do**: Spargel dinners, Rheingau wine tasting on warm weekends, blossom walks in Kurpark
**Skip**: international travel to places with high flight demand (Easter travel is crowded)

## May

**Spring/summer transition. Festival season starts.** Wiesbaden comes alive. Outdoor dining, wine festivals, concerts.

- May 1 (Labour Day): public holiday; no retail
- Muttertag (Mother's Day): 2nd Sunday
- Ascension Day: public holiday (40 days after Easter)
- Pfingsten (Pentecost): public holiday + Pfingstferien (1-week school break)
- Rheingau Gourmet Festival

**Do**: Rheingau wine events, Mainz Open Air Cathedral Market, hiking in Taunus
**Skip**: nothing; this is the best month

## June

**Full summer. Outdoor everything.** Wiesbaden's outdoor scene at full tilt.

- Corpus Christi (*Fronleichnam*): public holiday in Hessen (around mid-June)
- Summer solstice events
- Rheingau Musik Festival begins (runs through August)
- First strawberry season (*Erdbeerzeit*): strawberry stands everywhere

**Do**: Rheingau Musik Festival concerts at Schloss Vollrads etc., open-air cinema at Walhalla, Rhine river cruises
**Skip**: outdoor markets on peak weekends; they're mobbed

## July

**Hot, touristy, busy. Also: vacation exodus.** Germans leave on vacation; Wiesbaden is partly empty. Restaurants close for *Betriebsferien* (company holiday).

- School summer break begins mid-July
- Schirmherrin (city garden festival): early July
- Open-air opera in Frankfurt / Darmstadt

**Do**: Rhine boat trips, off-peak restaurant visits (they're not busy)
**Skip**: day trips to popular tourist spots (Rüdesheim is jammed); wine touring with kids (hot, crowded)

## August

**German vacation month. Much of the city shuts down.** Many small restaurants and businesses close for 2-4 weeks. The Wiesbaden rhythm slows.

- Weinwochen (Wine Weeks) on the Kurhaus lawn: 10 days of wine from Rheingau wineries, late August; early September
- Schützenfest in smaller towns (shooter's festivals)

**Do**: Weinwochen is the annual social event; plan around it; short getaways to quieter places; pool days at Thermen
**Skip**: trying to get anything done in early-mid August; half of Wiesbaden is on holiday

## September

**Peak Wiesbaden. The best month to live here.** Weather mild, crowds home from August, harvest season begins, schools back in session.

- Rheingau Weinwochen (continues from late August)
- Grape harvest (*Weinlese*) in Rheingau: mid-September onward
- Oktoberfest starts in Munich (despite the name, it's mid-September)
- Back-to-school for DoDEA

**Do**: every wine-country thing you've been meaning to do, Oktoberfest in Munich if you want, hiking in fall colors
**Skip**: trying to compete with Germans for weekend wine-country bookings; it's peak

## October

**Autumn. Golden light. Harvest festivals.** Grapes finishing, apples ready, chestnuts roasting.

- German Unity Day (3 October): public holiday
- *Herbstmarkt* (autumn market) in Wiesbaden downtown
- Oktoberfest in Munich ends first weekend of October
- *Martinstag* preparations begin

**Do**: Rheingau wine tasting (peak vintage tastings), pumpkin patches in Taunus, Oktoberfest parties
**Skip**: trying to ski; mountains aren't ready yet

## November

**Getting cold. Weihnachtsmarkt (Christmas market) prep.** Days short. Wrap-up season.

- Martinstag (11 Nov): children's lantern parades
- Totensonntag (Sunday before Advent): Memorial Day
- Sternschnuppenmarkt (Wiesbaden's Christmas market) begins late November
- First snow sometimes

**Do**: attend children's Martinstag parades, prepare for Christmas markets opening, Advent wreath workshops
**Skip**: outdoor hiking beyond light afternoons

## December

**Christmas-market season. Glühwein everywhere. December 25-26 are holidays.**

- Weihnachtsmärkte (Christmas markets) in Wiesbaden, Mainz, Frankfurt, Heidelberg, Nuremberg, etc.
- December 6: St. Nikolaus (kids get small gifts)
- December 24: Christmas Eve; stores close at noon; main family celebration
- December 25, 26: public holidays
- Silvester (New Year's Eve): fireworks; Germans actually buy their own

**Do**: multiple Christmas market trips (each town's is different), mulled wine tastings, hotel Christmas dinners
**Skip**: outdoor everything on Christmas Day (everything closed)

## Federal holidays to plan around (Hessen)

Days when retail closes and post-office / commissary typically closed:
- Jan 1: New Year's Day
- Good Friday + Easter Monday
- May 1: Labour Day
- Ascension Day (40 days after Easter)
- Whit Monday (Pfingstmontag, 50 days after Easter)
- Corpus Christi (Fronleichnam, May/June)
- Oct 3: German Unity Day
- Dec 25: Christmas Day
- Dec 26: Boxing Day / Second Christmas Day

DoDEA has its own US-based holiday calendar; German holidays apply to off-post life.

## Travel seasons

- **Best weather for European travel**: April-June, September-early October
- **Worst for weather-dependent trips**: November-March (except ski destinations)
- **School holidays**: Hessen school breaks for Pfingsten (May/June), Summer (Jul-Aug), Autumn (October), Christmas, Easter; German families book everything, prices spike
- **Cheapest European travel**: January, November (not Christmas), mid-February
