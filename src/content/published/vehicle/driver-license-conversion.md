## What

Two tracks to drive legally in Germany long-term. Most SOFA-status personnel use the **USAREUR Certificate of License** (on-post track). Family members without SOFA status, long-term civilian employees, and anyone planning to stay past PCS use the **German Führerschein conversion** (off-post track).

Both start with a valid US license. The paperwork diverges.

## Track 1 — USAREUR Certificate of License (on-post)

The standard option. Required for POV registration through DES Wiesbaden. Valid only on US installations and for SOFA-status driving in Germany.

**What you need**:
- Valid US driver license (not expired)
- Passing USAREUR driver written test — covers German road rules, signs, priority, Autobahn conduct
- Vision screening on-post
- ID card / CAC

**Where**:
USAREUR Driver Testing Center Wiesbaden (LRC-Wiesbaden). Appointments booked through the installation driver's testing office.

**Process**:
1. Pick up or download the USAREUR Drivers Testing study guide. Study it. The test is not trivial — Germany has right-of-way rules that don't exist in the US (e.g., priority from the right at uncontrolled intersections).
2. Schedule the test. Bring documents.
3. Pass the test — you walk out with a USAREUR Certificate of License (paper certificate, not a card).
4. Carry the certificate with your US driver's license.

**Cost**: free. Retakes are free but limited.

**Validity**: duration of your tour or 3 years, whichever is shorter. Renew before PCS extension.

## Track 2 — German Führerschein (off-post)

A full German driver's license, valid throughout the EU. Some US states have reciprocity with Germany — you can convert your license **without retesting**. Other states require the full German driving exam (theory + practical), which is expensive and time-consuming.

**Reciprocity map**:
- **Full reciprocity** (no test): AL, AR, AZ, CO, DE, ID, IL, IA, KS, KY, LA, MA, MI, MN, MO, MT, NE, NM, OH, OK, PA, PR, SC, SD, TN, TX, UT, VA, WA, WV, WI, WY, CA (partial)
- **Partial reciprocity** (theory only, no practical): AK, CT, DC, FL, GA, HI, IN, ME, MD, NV, NH, NJ, NC, ND, OR, RI, VT
- **No reciprocity** (full retest required): NY, MS

If your home-of-record state has full reciprocity, conversion is paperwork only.

**Where**:
**Fahrerlaubnisbehörde Wiesbaden** (Driving License Authority) — inside the Rathaus / Bürgeramt complex. Separate queue from Anmeldung.

**What you need**:
1. Valid US driver license (must be issued before you Anmelden)
2. Anmeldebescheinigung (Wiesbaden registration confirmation)
3. Passport
4. Biometric passport photo (German format — photo booths at the Rathaus or H&M across the street)
5. Vision test certificate (from a German optician or on-post driving test center; valid 2 years)
6. First-aid course certificate (ASB or DRK course, ~€30–50, 7-9 hours; required by German law)
7. Application form (Antrag auf Umschreibung) — filled at the Rathaus or online in advance
8. Fee: approximately €43 plus €0.90–€30 processing depending on reciprocity

**Processing time**: 4–8 weeks from submission. You surrender your US license to the German authority (they stamp "license exchanged" and forward it back to your home-state DMV after the German one is issued). Some states let you re-obtain the US license after PCS; others mark it as "exchanged abroad."

## Which track to pick

- **Active-duty Soldiers** who'll PCS out of Germany: USAREUR Certificate. You'll reclaim your US license on departure.
- **DA Civilians** serving long tours: consider the Führerschein — valid EU-wide and doesn't expire like the USAREUR cert.
- **Family members** staying long-term (e.g., spouse working in Germany): Führerschein. You may need to drive off-post fleets where USAREUR cert isn't recognized.
- **Dual US/EU citizens**: Führerschein, always. Some EU member states question the USAREUR cert at border checks.
- **Anyone who might buy a German-spec vehicle (not shipping a US POV)**: Führerschein. German-plated cars want German-licensed drivers for insurance purposes.

## Gotchas

- **Under SOFA, US license is valid in Germany for 6 months** from entry. After that, you need either the USAREUR cert or a Führerschein. Drive without one after the 6-month mark and you're unlicensed — ticket + points + German insurance voids.
- **First-aid course is not optional** for the Führerschein conversion. It's a German law requirement, not a US one. Book early.
- **Photos must be German biometric format**, not US passport format. They're different (neutral expression, specific dimensions). Photo booths at the Rathaus do them right; H&M and dm drugstores also have them.
- **You cannot convert a Führerschein back to a US license automatically.** When you leave Germany, your US state of residence decides whether to re-issue. Some states (Virginia, California) re-issue without test; others require a new driving test.
- **Motorcycle licenses convert separately.** Class A/A1/A2 requires a separate German motorcycle license process.
- **No reciprocity states (NY, MS)** face full retest costs: ~€1,500–€2,500 in theory + practical lessons, exam fees, and time. Sometimes Family members pre-emptively switch their home state before PCS to ease this.

## If you only need to drive occasionally

Don't convert. Drive on your US license + USAREUR cert. Use the shuttle for on-post trips, Deutsche Bahn for anything significant off-post. Many Family members never drive in Germany at all — the train system makes it optional.
