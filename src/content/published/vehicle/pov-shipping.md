## What

If you're command-sponsored accompanied, you're entitled to ship one POV at government expense (married accompanied may ship a second at personal expense). You drop it off at your losing Vehicle Processing Center (VPC) before you fly; you pick it up at Bremerhaven or Emden on the German end.

Timeline from drop-off to pickup is **4-8 weeks**. Plan accordingly; that's why rental-car budget matters.

## What actually happens

1. **2-4 weeks before your RDD**: schedule your POV drop-off at your losing VPC via PCSMyPOV.com. Cars drop-off requires drained fuel (under ¼ tank), cleaned interior, spare set of keys, and the VPC's paperwork.
2. **Drop off**: they inspect for existing damage, you sign. You walk out carless. You'll want rental-car coverage on your existing insurance, or swap to a US rental.
3. **In transit**: 3-6 weeks of ship-time to Bremerhaven or Emden.
4. **Arrival notification**: email or phone when the car hits the port. You have 30 days to pick it up before storage fees start.
5. **Pick up**: fly/train to Bremerhaven or Emden, or use the one-time rental car entitlement Army Lodge can set up. Bring SOFA card, orders, ID, and proof of insurance on the German policy (USAA writes USAREUR-specific coverage; VOB also works).
6. **Drive it home**: 4-6 hours to Wiesbaden depending on traffic. A 90-day temporary "2A" plate is issued at the VPC; you have 90 days to register through Wiesbaden DES.

## Gotchas

- **Clean it.** German customs rejects dirty cars; literal mud on the undercarriage gets you sent to a wash bay and billed. Hit a commercial car wash before VPC drop-off.
- **Drain the fuel to under ¼ tank.** Cars ship on cargo ships; fuel above the line violates maritime shipping rules.
- **Spare keys.** The VPC needs one set; keep one. Don't lose them; ordering a new key for a US car in Germany is expensive and slow.
- **USAA ships electronically.** VOB sends insurance confirmation to the German RMV electronically. Other US insurers don't; you'll have to navigate coverage manually.
- **Convertibles, motorcycles, classic cars** have additional paperwork. Check with the losing VPC.
- **Your POV entitlement is tied to orders, not to travel.** If you change orders mid-PCS, you may lose the entitlement. Confirm with Finance.
- **Customs at port**: you may be charged VAT on imported vehicles if paperwork is wrong. The VPC has forms for tax-free import under SOFA; make sure those are filled out at drop-off.
- **Fuel type**: most German gas stations are diesel-default. US gas cars take "Super" (95 octane) or "Super Plus" (98). Don't guess at the pump.

## Alternative: sell in CONUS, buy in Germany

Worth considering if:
- Your US car is old or fuel-inefficient (German gas runs ~€1.70/L = ~$7/gal)
- You don't want to pay to ship a depreciating asset
- You want a manual transmission (easier on narrow streets, cheaper to buy here)

Buying in Germany: AAFES Exchange has a US-spec car lot; local dealers around Wiesbaden sell both US-spec and German-spec. German-spec is cheaper but you can't take it back to CONUS without heavy modification. Plan accordingly.

## POV limits in USAREUR

- **Single or unaccompanied**: 2 POVs registrable at DES
- **Married accompanied**: 3 POVs
- More than that requires a Commander-signed waiver. Yes, really.
