## What

A **Kita** (*Kindertagesstätte*) is Germany's equivalent of daycare plus preschool, bundled. Most serve **Krippe** (ages ~1-3) and **Kindergarten** (ages 3-6); some combine both. Programs run 5-9 hours a day, usually with hot lunch. Staff speak German to children. This is the single most effective way to get an American kid genuinely bilingual during a Wiesbaden tour.

DoDEA runs Pre-K at Aukamm and Hainerberg Elementary Schools for command-sponsored dependents starting the year before Kindergarten. Kita fills the gap **before** DoDEA Pre-K (infant through age 4) and also serves as an alternative for parents who want German immersion.

Kita is **not** the same as CYS's Child Development Center (CDC). CDC is on-post, English-primary, MWR-operated. Kita is off-post, German-primary, municipal or church-affiliated.

## Where

Wiesbaden has roughly 150 Kitas spread across the city. Types:

- **Städtisch** (municipal); run by Stadt Wiesbaden. Cheapest, longest waitlists.
- **Kirchlich** (church-affiliated); Protestant (*Evangelisch*) and Catholic (*Katholisch*) operators, often with Christian programming but no doctrinal requirement.
- **Freier Träger** (independent); non-profit associations (AWO, Kinderschutzbund, parent co-ops).
- **Betriebskita** (employer-run); some employers run Kitas for staff. Outside reach for most military Families unless a spouse works at Mainz University, for example.
- **Private**; rare in Wiesbaden; more common for Kita-equivalents with international focus (English/German bilingual, Montessori, Waldorf).

Kitas exist within walking distance of most Wiesbaden neighborhoods. Off-post housing near Biebrich, Dotzheim, Bierstadt, Naurod, and Mainz-Kastel all have nearby options. On-post housing at Hainerberg or Aukamm puts you farther from many Kitas; driving is usually required.

## Registration process

### 1. Anmelden first
Kita registration depends on having registered residency (**Anmeldung**) at the Bürgeramt. You list the child as dependent and get their **Steueridentifikationsnummer** and Anmeldebestätigung. See [Anmeldung](/entries/legal/anmeldung) for that process.

### 2. Start early
Place your name on the central **Kitaportal Wiesbaden** waitlist **6-18 months before** you need the spot. Popular Kitas in desirable neighborhoods have two-year waitlists. Less-popular church Kitas can sometimes place within 3 months.

### 3. List multiple options
You can rank up to ~5 Kitas on the Kitaportal. Rank realistically; you're more likely to get the #3 or #4 choice than the top.

### 4. Visit ("Schnupperbesuch")
Kitas expect parents to visit before accepting a spot. Call to arrange a *Schnupperbesuch* (trial visit); 30-60 minutes, parent and child, meet the Erzieher\*innen (educators), look at the space.

### 5. Sign the Betreuungsvertrag
Once offered a spot, you sign a **Betreuungsvertrag** (care contract) that specifies hours, fees, pick-up authorizations, and notice periods. Most contracts require 2 months' notice to cancel.

### 6. Eingewöhnung (phased entry)
Most Kitas use a **Berliner Eingewöhnungsmodell**; 2-4 weeks where a parent stays, gradually phasing out. Plan leave accordingly; a week of unpaid leave is realistic for parents who can't telework.

## Cost

Hessen funds Kita heavily. For children **age 3 to school age**, Hessen's *Gutes-KiTa-Gesetz* implementation covers roughly 6 hours per day of care free of charge (the exact hours-free threshold is set annually; confirm with the specific Kita). You still pay for:

- **Lunch** (*Essengeld*); ~€60-90/month
- **Material fee**; modest, annually
- **Extended hours** beyond the 6 free hours; income-scaled; sliding scale

For **under-3** care (Krippe), full fees apply, income-scaled. Monthly bills vary from €100 (lower income) to €400+ (higher income) in Wiesbaden for full-day Krippe. Well below US daycare rates.

## Is a SOFA-status family eligible?

**Yes.** Hessen does not exclude SOFA-status Family members from Kita. Anmeldung establishes residency for Kita eligibility; your Steueridentifikationsnummer for the child is issued at Anmeldung.

Some Kitas list "no APO addresses" on forms; that's usually a technical limitation (German postal system), not an eligibility block. Use your German residential address on all Kita paperwork.

## What to expect

- **German-primary environment.** Your child will be the one who doesn't speak German for 2-6 months. This is fine. Bilingual children catch up rapidly.
- **Pedagogy is play-based.** No worksheets, no academic drills before age 5. Lots of outdoor time, cooking projects, nature days.
- **Food is real.** Most Kitas cook on-site; kids sit for lunch, learn table manners.
- **Outside regardless of weather.** Germans say "there is no bad weather, only bad clothing." Your kid needs a *Matschhose* (puddle-suit), good rain jacket, warm boots, and hat/gloves.
- **Parent involvement.** Monthly *Elternabend* (parents' evening), occasional field-trip help, seasonal festivals (St. Martin lantern walk, Fasching, summer fest). Basic German helps; English-speaking parents figure it out through other parents.

## Bilingual and international options

- **Internationale Montessori Schule Wiesbaden**; bilingual, private, tuition applies
- **English Playgroup Wiesbaden**; casual, not full Kita
- **Bi-Lingual Kinderhaus** types exist, usually private, waitlisted

For full English-primary care, the DoDEA Pre-K at Aukamm/Hainerberg Elementary Schools or on-post CDC (CYS) are the straightforward paths.

## Gotchas

- **Start before arrival.** Submit the Kitaportal preferences from CONUS if you can; the Stadt accepts remote preliminaries if you commit to Anmelden on arrival.
- **Don't count on a spot.** Plan for CDC as your fallback if Kita doesn't come through in time. Many Families use CDC for months then switch to Kita when a spot opens.
- **Summer Schließzeiten**: most Kitas close for 2-3 weeks in summer (often July or August). Parents cover with Family visits, leave, or drop-in care.
- **2 months' notice to cancel**; if you PCS, give notice as soon as orders are firm. Late cancellation can cost a month's fee.
- **Cold season attendance policies**: Kitas quarantine sick kids aggressively. Fever rule is typically 24-48 hours fever-free before return. Plan backup childcare.
- **Kita to DoDEA Kindergarten transition**: most Families transition at age 5 for English-primary K at DoDEA. Kita friendships usually stay; playdates and family-level connections continue.
- **Language shock is temporary.** Kids are frustrated for 1-2 months, then proud. Parents struggle longer with German paperwork. Google Translate and patient Erzieher\*innen help.
- **Religious Kitas** don't require church membership but do include seasonal religious programming. Ask during the Schnupperbesuch if you want to weigh this.
