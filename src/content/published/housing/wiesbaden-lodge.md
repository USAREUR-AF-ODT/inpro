## What

The **Wiesbaden Army Lodge** is the on-post temporary-lodging facility (TLF) for newly-arrived PCS families and for retirees or space-A travelers passing through. Rooms range from studio to multi-bedroom suites, many with kitchenettes. Rates track the DoS per-diem for Wiesbaden (currently around $195/night for lodging, adjusted seasonally).

For PCS families, the Lodge is usually the landing spot for the first 2–10 weeks while you clear in-processing, sign on-post quarters, or close on a German rental. **Temporary Lodging Allowance (TLA)** is the entitlement that reimburses most of the Lodge cost, and the Lodge is experienced at handling the paperwork.

See [TLA reality](/entries/finance/tla-reality) for the finance side and [Housing areas compared](/entries/housing/housing-areas-compared) for where you'll eventually live.

## Where

**Hainerberg Housing — Mississippi Strasse 45, BLDG 7890, Wiesbaden 65189.** Walking distance to:

- **PX / Exchange** and **Commissary** (Hainerberg main complex)
- **Wiesbaden Entertainment Center** (food, bowling, events)
- **DoDEA Hainerberg Elementary** and **Middle School**
- **Hainerberg Shoppette** and gas station

Roughly **30 minutes by car from Frankfurt International Airport (FRA)**. Wiesbaden Hauptbahnhof is a **20-minute walk** or a short taxi ride; S-Bahn and regional trains connect from Hbf to Frankfurt, Mainz, and beyond.

## When

- **Front desk** — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
- **Check-in** — generally 1500; early check-in subject to room availability
- **Check-out** — 1100; late check-out by arrangement
- **Breakfast** ("Sunrise Start") — included in the room rate for registered guests; Mon–Fri 0600–0900, weekends and US federal holidays 0800–1000

Peak demand: May–August PCS season. Reservations 4–12 weeks ahead are typical during this window.

## Rates and TLA mechanics

### Rates
- **Set by per-diem** — DoS publishes the Wiesbaden lodging per diem; Lodge rates shift when per diem shifts
- **Seasonal variation** — summer higher, winter lower in recent years
- **Children under 12** typically stay free in the same room as a parent; Lodge confirms specifics at booking
- **Pets** — ask at reservation; pet rooms exist but limited and with additional nightly fee

### TLA coverage
**Temporary Lodging Allowance (TLA)** reimburses ADFMs and DA Civilians for lodging and meal costs on PCS. Key facts:

- **Max 60 days** on arrival TLA; extensions to 90+ possible with command approval
- **Per diem-capped** — lodging and meals together are capped at the published per-diem rate minus a subsistence offset
- **Requires a TLA claim** filed with Finance, supported by itemized Lodge receipts
- **Does not reimburse you in real time** — you front the money, get paid after claim processing
- **Your clock starts the day you check in** to any TLF, including the Lodge

See [TLA reality](/entries/finance/tla-reality) for the filing process and common snags.

### If the Lodge is full
MWR contracts with nearby off-post hotels (typically within Wiesbaden) as overflow. Overflow rates are also TLA-covered but vary; always confirm the specific hotel is TLA-approved before booking.

## Amenities

- **Rooms** — studio and multi-room suites; kitchenettes with refrigerator, microwave, cooktop in most units
- **Free wifi** throughout
- **DSN telephone access** in rooms
- **Wireless printing** at the front desk
- **Laundry facilities** on-site
- **Business center** with internet and printer access
- **Kids' play area** (indoor) and outdoor recreation space
- **Free breakfast** for registered guests
- **Parking** on-site

Linens and weekly housekeeping included. Self-service is expected for long stays — bring or buy your own dishwashing soap, toilet paper after the first week, etc.

## Booking flow

1. **Decide on dates** — estimate check-in and check-out. Overshoot the check-out; it's easier to check out early than extend during peak season.
2. **Download the reservation form** from the Lodge page on the MWR portal.
3. **Fill out** your personal info, arrival details, family members, and any special needs (crib, accessible room, pets).
4. **Email to reservations@army.mil** — subject line "Reservation Request [Last Name] [Arrival Date]."
5. **Confirmation** comes back by email within 1–3 business days. Keep the confirmation number; it's your reference for all Lodge interactions.
6. **On arrival**: walk up to the front desk with your military ID, PCS orders, and confirmation. Check-in is usually fast.

## Gotchas

- **Peak-season booking** — Memorial Day to Labor Day fills up. Book **4 weeks minimum** before arrival; 8–12 weeks is safer.
- **TLA clock starts at check-in** at any TLF. If you arrive early before your sponsor or in-processing is ready, you burn TLA days waiting. Arrive close to your report date when possible.
- **Rate changes mid-stay** are possible if per diem resets. Lodge will notify but not always before billing.
- **Kitchenettes save money on meal TLA** — cook groceries from the commissary, file receipts, get reimbursed on the TLA side.
- **Laundry** — some rooms have in-unit; others send you to a shared facility. Confirm at booking if it matters for you.
- **Pets**: the Lodge does accept pets in designated rooms. Specify at reservation. There is a pet fee, and some size/species limits apply. Bring your pet's DEERS-linked vaccination records.
- **Check-out receipts** — get the itemized breakdown of lodging vs. tax; your TLA claim needs it. Replacement receipts after the fact are a hassle.
- **Extensions beyond 60 days** require a formal TLA extension request through your unit. Start the paperwork 2 weeks before you need the extension.
- **The Lodge is on Hainerberg, not Clay.** Most in-processing happens on Clay Kaserne. Factor in a 15-minute drive (or AAFES shuttle bus during peak times) for appointments.
- **Parking** is included but a specific vehicle pass may be issued on arrival; display it to avoid tow.
- **Front-desk language** is English; staff handle German paperwork on your behalf during in-processing as needed.
