## What

Shipping your stuff to Germany breaks into three categories with very different timelines:

- **HHG (Household Goods)**; the bulk of your possessions (furniture, most boxes). **Ships by sea container**. Arrival 6-12 weeks from pickup.
- **Unaccompanied Baggage (UB)**; priority items you want sooner (kitchen basics, bedding, a few clothes, kids' essentials). **Air cargo**. Arrival 3-4 weeks from pickup.
- **Express / Expedited Items**; urgent items (laptop, critical documents). **Air express**. Arrival 1 week from pickup.

Your weight allowance depends on rank + dependents. A typical accompanied O-3 gets ~10,000 lbs total HHG+UB+express combined, with UB capped at 600 lbs.

## The phase-by-phase game plan

### 8-12 weeks before PCS
- Get your PCS briefing from your losing post's Personal Property Office
- Decide what ships vs. sells vs. stores
- Schedule pack-out dates (usually 3 separate days: UB, then HHG, then express)

### Pack-out strategy
- **UB (air cargo, 30 days)**: think "first month of apartment living"; basic pots/pans, sheets/blankets for each bed, 2 weeks of clothes, children's comfort items, work laptop, basic toiletries. Maximum 600 lbs typically.
- **HHG (sea, 60-90 days)**: everything else. Furniture, bulk clothes, books, decor, sports gear, garage stuff.
- **Express (air, 7 days)**: business essentials and anything life-critical. Laptop, meds, birth certificates, wedding photos.

### What NOT to ship
- Weapons (separate process, check with losing LRC)
- Alcoholic beverages (legally shippable but quantity-restricted)
- Perishables
- Hazmat (paint, propane, batteries)
- Plants / fresh plants
- Cash / jewelry (keep with you)

### What the packers pack
The moving company packs and boxes your stuff. You should:
- **Be present** every day of pack-out
- **Photograph everything** before and during packing; dent on the sofa, scratch on the dresser, contents of each box
- **Inventory the inventory**; the packers generate a numbered inventory; check every box number matches your photo record
- **Mark "UB" vs "HHG"** clearly for each room/category
- **Keep valuables with you**; don't let a ring or a laptop go into HHG

### Pack-out day gotchas
- Some packers will try to pack anything; pantry food, trash, dirty laundry. Remove anything you don't want shipped before they arrive.
- Don't let them pack fluids unless you want leaking boxes in sea freight.
- The inventory tag on each box matters; if an inventory tag is missing, customs can flag the entire shipment.

## On the German end

### Arrival notification
LRC-Wiesbaden contacts you when your shipment arrives at port (Bremerhaven for HHG, FRA for air). You schedule delivery to your permanent residence.

### Scheduling delivery
- **UB**: delivery 2-7 days after customs clearance
- **HHG**: delivery 5-14 days after customs clearance
- **Express**: same-day or next-day after clearance

You (or your sponsor, or ACS, or a household member) must be present at delivery. Packers will unpack to the first room; they won't put clothes on hangers or dishes in cabinets.

### Damage claims
Damage happens. File claims through the moving company's claim system:
- **90-day window** to file basic claim from delivery date
- **2-year window** to file full claim with itemized damage
- **Documentation required**: photos of damage, receipts for repair estimates, the original packing inventory numbers

The process is bureaucratic. ACS has a Personal Property advocate who can help.

## TLA and HHG interact

TLA (Temporary Lodging Allowance) is designed to cover the gap between arriving and when HHG arrives. If HHG is delayed beyond 30 days from arrival, you can **extend TLA at a reduced rate for up to 30 additional days post-move-in** to cover eating-out costs.

Don't start your TLA clock if your UB is about to arrive and you can move in; the UB is usually enough to survive on during the HHG wait.

## On-post housing vs. off-post

- **On-post**: partially furnished in most cases; beds, dining table, basic living room furniture. You ship less HHG.
- **Off-post**: unfurnished is the German default. You ship more, or buy furniture locally (IKEA Eschborn is 20 min away).

Check with HSO about your specific housing assignment before the pack-out; knowing whether you get a bed affects your UB packing list.

## Gotchas

- **Pack-out takes 1-3 days.** Don't assume it's a half-day exercise. Plan to be home.
- **You pay for what gets damaged / lost**; keep original receipts for high-value items (TVs, electronics). Replacement requires them.
- **"Professional Book, Papers, and Equipment" (PBP&E)**; job-related items (your Army gear, work laptop for DA Civs) don't count against weight allowance. Document them separately.
- **Plants, pets, plant-based items don't ship.** Sell/give the succulents before pickup.
- **"Dirty Moves"**; your HHG gets sealed at your old house and opened at your new one. Customs may inspect randomly; have nothing to hide.
- **Oversize items** (pianos, gun safes) require special handling; coordinate with losing LRC 2+ weeks before pack-out.
- **Crating vs. boxing**; wood-crated items (pianos, large art) cost more but protect better. Worth it for heirlooms.
- **Couch-size furniture may not fit into German apartments**; narrow doorways, tiny elevators. Measure doorways + stairwells of your likely German apartment before shipping a 10-foot sectional.
- **Ship things that plug into 110V**; German outlets are 220V. Your US toaster won't work here. Transformers help for a few appliances but not high-draw items.
