## What

Under **AR 600-8-8 (Total Army Sponsorship Program)**, every soldier and DA Civilian PCSing to a new permanent duty station is entitled to a sponsor. The gaining unit owns the assignment; the losing unit can nudge, but cannot assign for them. The actual workflow varies by unit; some assign sponsors within days of receiving your orders, others drop the ball for months.

If you are **90 days from your Report No Later Than Date (RNLTD)** and don't yet have a sponsor, it's time to push. The system works, but you usually have to work it.

## Where

- **Sponsor assignment** lives with your **gaining unit's S1**; the S1 section of the unit you'll report to, not USAG Wiesbaden garrison
- **TASP portal**; Army sponsorship enrollment and tracking tool; accessed via Army365 SharePoint
- **USAG Wiesbaden Sponsorship office**; backstop under DFMWR's ACS; can nudge unit S1s and arrange spouse-side sponsors

## When

- **T-90 days from RNLTD**: if no sponsor assigned, start the request
- **T-60 days**: sponsor should be assigned and in contact with you
- **T-45 days**: if still no sponsor, escalate
- **T-30 days**: at this point you absorb the cost of not having a sponsor; keep pushing, but plan for self-service arrival

## Contact

For sponsor-assignment questions:

- **Your losing-unit S1**; first call, can query the gaining unit for status
- **Gaining-unit S1**; email directly using the unit's published roster or the orders-issuing office
- **USAG Wiesbaden ACS Relocation Readiness**; provides sponsorship backup coordination
- **USAG Wiesbaden Command Sergeant Major's office**; escalation for soldier-side gaps
- **Gaining-unit CSM/1SG**; enlisted soldiers can email directly; officer-to-officer email for officers

Phone directory: see the USAG Wiesbaden phone directory on home.army.mil/wiesbaden, and your unit's specific S1 contact on its home.army.mil tenant page.

## Step-by-step

### 1. Identify your gaining unit
Your orders list the UIC and unit designation. Cross-reference against the USAG Wiesbaden tenant-unit list ([USAG Wiesbaden tenant units](/entries/unit/tenant-units)) to confirm which command you're joining.

### 2. Find the gaining unit S1
Most Wiesbaden tenant units (66th MI, 2nd Signal Bde, V Corps Forward, 5th Signal Command, etc.) have S1 contact info on their unit page under home.army.mil/wiesbaden → Units/Tenants → [your unit]. Enterprise email format is usually firstname.mi.lastname.mil@army.mil.

### 3. Send the initial email
Subject: "PCS Arrival; [Rank Last Name]; RNLTD [date]"

Body:
- Your rank, last name, MOS/AOC
- Your orders number and RNLTD
- Family situation (accompanied/unaccompanied, number/ages of dependents, pets, POV)
- Expected travel window
- Question: "Could you please assign a sponsor? I'd like to make contact before arrival."

Attach a redacted copy of your orders (SSN redacted).

### 4. Enroll in TASP
**Total Army Sponsorship Program (TASP)** is the tracking tool. Log in with your CAC from any Army365 connection; enroll yourself under "Incoming Soldier." This creates a record that mirrors your email, which helps when escalation is needed later. Not every unit uses TASP actively, but the paper trail matters.

### 5. Give it 2 weeks
Units are busy; 2 weeks is reasonable for a reply. If nothing:

- **Follow up** with a short "following up on my previous email" reminder
- **Loop in the losing-unit S1** to query from their side (losing S1s often have enterprise-facing access to gaining UICs)

### 6. Escalate if still silent at T-45
- **Email the gaining unit's CSM or 1SG.** Short, professional, factual. "Sir/Sergeant Major, I've emailed the S1 at [date] and [date] requesting a sponsor for my PCS to [unit], RNLTD [date]. I have not received a reply. Requesting your assistance."
- **Copy your losing-unit chain** so they know you're escalating with cause.
- **Use the TASP record** to attach your paper trail.

### 7. ACS backup
At T-30 if nothing works, contact **USAG Wiesbaden ACS Relocation Readiness**. ACS can:

- Pair you with a volunteer sponsor (often a spouse or fellow soldier in your housing area) independent of unit
- Coordinate with the garrison CSM's office to put pressure on the unit S1
- Schedule you directly into the Newcomer's Orientation and Welcome Packet so you're not starting from zero

ACS volunteer sponsors are usually highly engaged; the people who raise their hand for volunteer sponsorship are often the best sponsors you'll meet.

## Special cases

### DA Civilians
DA Civilians are entitled to sponsorship under AR 600-8-8 and DoD 1400.25-V792. Gaining civilian personnel office (CPAC at USAG Wiesbaden) coordinates. Email CPAC if your gaining organization hasn't assigned one. Civilian sponsors are often assigned within the incoming employee's directorate or tenant unit.

### NAF employees
Separate process through NAF HR; ACS can help bridge if assignment lags.

### Contractors
Not entitled to assigned sponsorship. Informal unit volunteers or ACS can substitute. Your contract company may assign a workplace-side point of contact, which is adjacent to but different from Army sponsorship.

### Spouse sponsorship
Spouses request independently through **ACS Spouse Sponsorship** or the unit FRG. The soldier's assigned sponsor usually covers the family, but if you want a separate spouse-specific contact (especially for spouses in EFMP or with specialized career/school needs), request one.

### Reassignment / short-notice PCS
Sponsors are required regardless of notice time. For short-notice PCS (<60 days), use ACS and push the gaining S1 hard. The ACS Newcomer's Orientation can substitute for some sponsor value if the window is too short.

## What makes a good escalation email

- **Professional, factual, dated.** "I emailed 2025-08-15 and 2025-08-29 requesting a sponsor."
- **Attach paper trail.** Include your prior emails, TASP screenshot.
- **State the ask clearly.** "Requesting your assistance in expediting sponsor assignment."
- **Don't editorialize.** No "I'm disappointed" or "this is unacceptable." Facts land; emotion doesn't.
- **Short.** 4-6 sentences. Chain of command reads on mobile.

## Gotchas

- **Don't assume "no reply" means "no sponsor."** Sometimes assignment happens and the email just hasn't landed. Call once to confirm before escalating.
- **Enterprise email delays.** S1 emails sometimes bounce between .mail.mil addresses; use the Army White Pages to find the current active email if your first try fails.
- **OCONUS S1 phone coverage is patchy.** Calling 0300 your time is often their 0900 duty day. Be realistic.
- **Bad sponsors aren't always fixable.** If you're assigned a sponsor who never replies, ACS can supplement with a volunteer. The formal reassignment is harder; ask the S1 if a swap is possible, but don't expect one.
- **Officer-to-officer requests** for senior grades (O-4 and above) sometimes go to the gaining unit XO instead of S1. Match your rank to the right echelon.
- **Don't bypass chain.** Emailing the CG or brigade commander before the S1 has been given reasonable time makes you look impatient, not serious.
- **TASP doesn't replace direct contact.** TASP is paper trail; real progress happens in email and phone calls.
